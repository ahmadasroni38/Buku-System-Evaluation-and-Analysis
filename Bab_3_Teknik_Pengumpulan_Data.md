# TEKNIK PENGUMPULAN DATA

## Pendahuluan

Pengumpulan data merupakan fondasi vital dalam setiap proses evaluasi sistem informasi. Kualitas data yang dikumpulkan secara langsung menentukan validitas, reliabilitas, dan kegunaan hasil evaluasi. Dalam konteks evaluasi sistem informasi, evaluator menghadapi tantangan unik: sistem informasi bersifat multidimensional, melibatkan aspek teknis, organisasional, dan manusia yang saling berinteraksi dalam ekosistem yang kompleks. Oleh karena itu, pemilihan dan penerapan teknik pengumpulan data yang tepat menjadi keterampilan krusial yang harus dikuasai oleh setiap evaluator.

Bab ini menguraikan berbagai teknik pengumpulan data yang telah terbukti efektif dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, mulai dari instrumen survei yang terstandarisasi, wawancara terstruktur yang mendalam, observasi partisipatif yang kontekstual, hingga analisis log sistem yang objektif. Setiap teknik memiliki kekuatan dan keterbatasan tersendiri, serta cocok untuk menjawab jenis pertanyaan evaluasi yang berbeda. Pemahaman komprehensif tentang berbagai teknik ini memungkinkan evaluator untuk merancang strategi pengumpulan data yang robust, menggunakan multiple methods untuk triangulasi, dan pada akhirnya menghasilkan insight yang valid dan actionable tentang sistem yang dievaluasi.

## 3.1 Instrumen Survei

Survei merupakan salah satu metode pengumpulan data yang paling populer dan versatile dalam evaluasi sistem informasi. Popularitas survei berakar pada kemampuannya untuk mengumpulkan data dari sampel besar secara efisien, menghasilkan data yang terstruktur dan mudah dianalisis secara statistik, serta memberikan representasi kuantitatif tentang persepsi, sikap, dan pengalaman pengguna sistem. Dalam era digital, survei online telah menjadi instrumen standar untuk mengukur kepuasan pengguna, mengidentifikasi masalah usability, menilai kualitas layanan sistem, dan mengumpulkan feedback untuk perbaikan sistem.

Efektivitas survei dalam evaluasi sistem informasi bergantung pada desain instrumen yang cermat, sampling yang representatif, dan analisis yang rigorous. Survei yang dirancang dengan baik dapat menghasilkan insight yang dalam tentang bagaimana sistem dipersepsikan dan digunakan oleh berbagai stakeholder, mengidentifikasi gap antara ekspektasi dan pengalaman aktual, serta menyediakan baseline metrics untuk monitoring kinerja sistem dari waktu ke waktu. Namun, survei juga memiliki keterbatasan inheren—terutama dalam menangkap nuansa kontekstual dan kompleksitas pengalaman pengguna—yang perlu dikelola melalui desain yang thoughtful dan kombinasi dengan metode pengumpulan data lainnya.

### 3.1.1 Desain Instrumen Survei yang Efektif

Desain instrumen survei yang efektif dimulai dengan klarifikasi tujuan evaluasi dan operasionalisasi konstruk yang akan diukur. Evaluator harus secara eksplisit mendefinisikan apa yang ingin diukur—apakah kepuasan pengguna keseluruhan, usability spesifik, kualitas informasi, atau perceived usefulness—dan bagaimana konstruk abstrak ini akan diterjemahkan ke dalam pertanyaan konkret yang dapat dijawab oleh responden. Proses operasionalisasi ini sering melibatkan review literatur untuk mengidentifikasi validated measures yang sudah ada, konsultasi dengan domain experts, dan pilot testing untuk memastikan bahwa pertanyaan dipahami sebagaimana dimaksud.

Struktur survei harus logical dan user-friendly, dimulai dengan pertanyaan yang mudah dan non-threatening untuk membangun rapport dan engagement responden, kemudian progressively bergerak ke pertanyaan yang lebih complex atau sensitive. Grouping pertanyaan berdasarkan tema atau dimensi konstruk membantu responden memfokuskan pemikiran mereka dan mengurangi cognitive load. Panjang survei harus carefully balanced—terlalu pendek mungkin tidak menangkap kompleksitas fenomena yang dievaluasi, tetapi terlalu panjang dapat menyebabkan survey fatigue dan menurunkan response rate serta kualitas respons.

Platform digital untuk survei seperti Google Forms, Microsoft Forms, Qualtrics, SurveyMonkey, atau LimeSurvey menawarkan fitur-fitur canggih yang dapat meningkatkan kualitas pengumpulan data. Conditional logic atau skip patterns memungkinkan personalisasi survei berdasarkan respons sebelumnya, meningkatkan relevance dan mengurangi beban responden. Randomization dari urutan pertanyaan atau pilihan jawaban dapat mengurangi bias respons. Validation rules memastikan completeness dan accuracy data—misalnya, memastikan bahwa alamat email diformat dengan benar atau bahwa sum dari persentase total 100%. Mobile optimization sangat krusial mengingat proporsi besar pengguna internet mengakses survei melalui smartphone.

Dalam konteks evaluasi sistem informasi di Indonesia, cultural sensitivity dalam desain survei sangat penting. Bahasa pertanyaan harus disesuaikan dengan level pendidikan dan technical literacy target responden. Istilah teknis yang unavoidable harus dijelaskan dengan contoh atau analogi yang familiar. Untuk survei yang ditujukan kepada diverse user population, bilingual questionnaires (Indonesia-Inggris) atau bahkan multilingual versions mungkin diperlukan. Consideration terhadap cultural norms—seperti tendency untuk social desirability bias atau reluctance untuk memberikan kritik langsung—harus diantisipasi dalam desain pertanyaan dan interpretasi hasil.

### 3.1.2 Jenis-jenis Pertanyaan Survei

Pertanyaan survei dapat dikategorisasi menjadi dua tipe utama: closed-ended dan open-ended, masing-masing dengan variations dan use cases yang spesifik. Closed-ended questions menyediakan set predefined response options yang responden pilih, memfasilitasi standardisasi respons dan analisis kuantitatif. Multiple choice questions cocok untuk categorical data seperti demografi (jenis kelamin, departemen, role) atau behavioral choices (sistem yang paling sering digunakan, waktu penggunaan). Dichotomous questions (yes/no, agree/disagree) efektif untuk binary decisions tetapi dapat oversimplify phenomena yang complex.

Likert scales adalah jenis pertanyaan yang paling umum dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, measuring level of agreement, frequency, satisfaction, atau importance pada ordinal scale—typically 5 atau 7 points. Misalnya: "Sejauh mana Anda setuju dengan pernyataan: 'Sistem ERP mudah digunakan untuk menyelesaikan tugas sehari-hari saya.'" dengan options dari 1 (Sangat Tidak Setuju) hingga 5 (Sangat Setuju). Odd-numbered scales menyediakan neutral midpoint, sementara even-numbered scales memaksa responden untuk lean ke satu sisi. Consistency dalam direction (apakah higher numbers always berarti more positive) penting untuk menghindari confusion dan response errors.

Semantic differential scales mengukur meaning of concepts dengan rating mereka pada bipolar adjective pairs, seperti "Antarmuka sistem: Rumit 1-2-3-4-5 Sederhana" atau "Performa sistem: Lambat 1-2-3-4-5 Cepat". Teknik ini particularly useful untuk measuring perceptions dan attitudes terhadap sistem secara holistic. Rating scales dapat menggunakan berbagai anchors—numerical (1-10), verbal (Poor-Fair-Good-Very Good-Excellent), atau visual (emoji faces dari sad hingga happy). Pilihan anchor harus matched dengan construct yang diukur dan familiar bagi target audience.

Open-ended questions memungkinkan responden untuk memberikan respons dalam kata-kata mereka sendiri, menghasilkan rich qualitative data yang dapat mengungkap issues yang tidak terantisipasi oleh evaluator. Contoh: "Apa kendala utama yang Anda hadapi saat menggunakan sistem e-learning?" atau "Saran apa yang Anda miliki untuk meningkatkan sistem?" Open-ended questions particularly valuable di akhir survei untuk capturing additional feedback yang tidak tertangkap oleh structured questions. Namun, respons open-ended memerlukan analisis qualitative yang time-intensive—coding, categorization, dan interpretation—yang harus dipertimbangkan dalam planning resources.

Dalam praktik evaluasi sistem informasi di organisasi Indonesia, kombinasi balanced antara closed-ended dan open-ended questions terbukti paling efektif. Closed-ended questions provide quantifiable metrics yang demanded oleh management untuk decision-making dan performance tracking. Open-ended questions memberikan contextual insights dan user voice yang essential untuk understanding "why" behind the numbers dan untuk identifying specific improvement opportunities yang actionable.

### 3.1.3 Validitas dan Reliabilitas Instrumen

Validitas instrumen survei mengacu pada sejauh mana instrumen mengukur apa yang seharusnya diukur—accuracy dan appropriateness dari conclusions yang dibuat berdasarkan survey scores. Content validity memastikan bahwa survey items comprehensively mewakili domain konstruk yang diukur. Misalnya, survei untuk mengukur "system quality" harus mencakup items yang represent berbagai dimensi quality seperti reliability, response time, availability, security, dan usability—tidak hanya fokus pada satu aspek. Content validity typically established melalui expert review dan literature-based item development.

Construct validity adalah extent dimana survey scores relate to other variables dalam cara yang consistent dengan theoretical predictions. Convergent validity demonstrated ketika measures dari same construct (misalnya, user satisfaction measured dengan different scales) highly correlated. Discriminant validity demonstrated ketika measures dari different constructs (misalnya, satisfaction vs. intention to use) show lower correlations. Factor analysis—exploratory atau confirmatory—sering digunakan untuk assess construct validity dengan examining apakah survey items load pada expected factors atau dimensions.

Criterion validity menguji apakah survey scores predict atau correlate dengan external criteria atau outcomes. Concurrent validity assessed dengan correlating survey scores dengan other measures collected at the same time—misalnya, korelasi antara perceived ease of use dan actual usage time recorded dalam system logs. Predictive validity assessed dengan examining apakah survey scores predict future outcomes—misalnya, apakah satisfaction scores pada implementasi awal predict long-term system adoption rates.

Reliabilitas mengacu pada consistency dan stability dari measurements—extent dimana survei produces same results ketika administered repeatedly under similar conditions. Internal consistency reliability, yang diukur dengan Cronbach's alpha coefficient, assesses extent dimana items dalam scale measure same construct. Cronbach's alpha values berkisar dari 0 hingga 1, dengan values ≥ 0.70 generally considered acceptable untuk research purposes dan ≥ 0.80 untuk applied decisions. Low alpha might indicate bahwa items measure different constructs atau bahwa items poorly written.

Test-retest reliability assessed dengan administering same survey kepada same respondents pada dua time points dan calculating correlation antara scores. High correlation indicates stability measurements over time. Namun, dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, user perceptions dapat legitimately berubah seiring waktu karena learning, system improvements, atau changing expectations—sehingga moderate test-retest reliability tidak necessarily indicate poor instrument quality. Inter-rater reliability relevant ketika survey respons require coding atau rating oleh multiple evaluators—misalnya, dalam categorizing open-ended responses.

Dalam konteks evaluasi sistem e-government di Indonesia, misalnya, validitas instrumen dapat ditingkatkan dengan adapting internationally validated scales (seperti SERVQUAL untuk service quality atau TAM untuk technology acceptance) dan conducting pilot testing dengan Indonesian users untuk ensure cultural appropriateness dan comprehensibility. Reliabilitas dapat dimonitor dengan calculating Cronbach's alpha untuk each dimension dan refining items yang contribute to low reliability.

### 3.1.4 Distribusi dan Pengumpulan Data Survei

Strategi distribusi survei significantly impacts response rate, sample representativeness, dan data quality. Email invitations dengan embedded survey link merupakan metode paling common untuk organizational surveys, offering personalization, tracking, dan reminder capabilities. Email harus clearly communicate purpose survei, estimated completion time (realistis—underselling dapat damage trust), assurance tentang confidentiality atau anonymity, dan deadline untuk completion. Personalized invitations dengan nama responden dan relevant context (misalnya, menyebutkan sistem spesifik yang mereka gunakan) tend to increase response rates dibanding generic mass emails.

Web-based survey panels atau pop-up surveys within systems dapat capture feedback pada point of use, when experience still fresh dalam user memory. Misalnya, sistem ERP dapat display brief satisfaction survey setelah user completes critical transaction. Timing dan frequency pop-ups harus carefully managed untuk avoid disrupting workflow dan causing annoyance—triggered surveys berdasarkan specific events (misalnya, setelah mencapai milestone atau encountering error) often lebih effective daripada random atau fixed-interval surveys.

Mobile-optimized surveys increasingly important mengingat prevalence smartphone usage. Responsive design ensures survei accessible dan usable across devices. Pertimbangan mobile-specific termasuk shorter survey length (mobile users have lower tolerance untuk lengthy surveys), larger touch targets untuk radio buttons dan checkboxes, vertical scrolling yang smooth, dan minimal text entry (using sliders, dropdowns, atau selection lists instead). Push notifications via mobile apps dapat complement email invitations untuk reminding users.

Sampling strategy menentukan siapa yang invited untuk participate dan how they selected. Probability sampling (simple random, stratified, cluster) provides basis untuk statistical generalization tetapi requires complete sampling frame. Stratified sampling ensures representation dari important subgroups—misalnya, stratifying berdasarkan department, role, atau experience level dengan sistem. Non-probability sampling (convenience, purposive, quota) more practical dalam many organizational settings tetapi limits generalizability. Clear documentation tentang sampling approach essential untuk interpreting results dan assessing limitations.

Response rate optimization critical untuk ensuring adequate sample size dan reducing non-response bias. Strategies include: multiple reminders (research shows 2-3 reminders dapat significantly boost response rates), optimal timing (avoiding busy periods, holidays, atau end-of-fiscal-year), incentives (monetary rewards, prize draws, atau non-monetary incentives seperti early access ke survey results), dan endorsement dari senior leadership atau respected figures. Monitoring response rates by subgroups dapat identify under-represented segments yang require targeted follow-up.

Dalam evaluasi sistem informasi akademik (SIAKAD) di universitas Indonesia, misalnya, distribusi survei dapat leverage multiple channels: email ke official student dan faculty addresses, announcements di learning management system, links di student portal, dan bahkan QR codes di kampus untuk mobile access. Collaboration dengan academic administrators untuk communicate importance participation dan potentially linking survey completion ke academic processes (dengan appropriate ethical safeguards) dapat boost response rates.

### 3.1.5 Analisis Respons Survei

Analisis respons survei dimulai dengan data cleaning dan preparation. Missing data inspection identifies patterns dalam non-response—apakah missing completely at random (MCAR), missing at random (MAR), atau missing not at random (MNAR). Strategies untuk handling missing data include listwise deletion (removing cases dengan any missing values, appropriate ketika data MCAR dan loss acceptable), pairwise deletion (using available data untuk each analysis), mean imputation (replacing missing dengan mean, simple tetapi dapat underestimate variance), atau multiple imputation (sophisticated technique yang preserves distributional properties). Outlier detection identifies extreme values yang mungkin indicate data entry errors atau unique cases yang warrant special attention.

Descriptive statistics provide initial understanding tentang data distributions dan response patterns. Frequencies dan percentages untuk categorical variables describe sample composition—misalnya, distribusi responden by department, role, atau experience level. Measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) dan dispersion (standard deviation, range) untuk continuous atau ordinal variables summarize typical responses dan variability. Cross-tabulations explore relationships antara categorical variables—misalnya, satisfaction levels by user role atau department.

Visualizations enhance comprehensibility dan communicate findings effectively. Bar charts atau column charts appropriate untuk comparing frequencies across categories. Pie charts show proportional breakdowns tetapi should be used sparingly (difficult untuk compare similar-sized segments). Histograms display distributions dari continuous variables. Box plots show median, quartiles, dan outliers, useful untuk comparing distributions across groups. Heatmaps dapat visualize patterns dalam correlation matrices atau cross-tabulations dengan many categories.

Inferential statistics test hypotheses dan generalize findings beyond sample. Chi-square tests examine associations antara categorical variables—misalnya, whether satisfaction differs significantly across departments. T-tests compare means antara dua groups—misalnya, comparing perceived usefulness antara novice dan experienced users. ANOVA extends comparison ke multiple groups. Correlation analysis (Pearson atau Spearman) examines relationships antara continuous variables—misalnya, relationship antara system quality dan user satisfaction. Regression analysis models relationships, allowing prediction dan identification dari significant predictors—misalnya, which system attributes most strongly predict overall satisfaction.

Dalam konteks evaluasi sistem ERP di perusahaan manufaktur Indonesia, analisis survei mungkin reveal bahwa users dari department finance report significantly higher satisfaction (mean = 4.2/5) dibanding users dari production department (mean = 3.1/5). Regression analysis mungkin show bahwa "ease of use" dan "system reliability" adalah strongest predictors of overall satisfaction, accounting untuk 68% variance. Cross-tabulation analysis mungkin identify bahwa users dengan less than 6 months experience disproportionately report difficulties, suggesting need untuk enhanced training programs. Analisis open-ended responses mungkin uncover specific pain points seperti "slow response time during peak hours" atau "lack of integration dengan existing production monitoring systems" yang tidak apparent dari quantitative data alone, providing actionable insights untuk system improvement.

## 3.2 Wawancara Terstruktur

Wawancara merupakan teknik pengumpulan data yang powerful untuk mengeksplorasi pengalaman, persepsi, dan pemahaman mendalam stakeholder tentang sistem informasi. Berbeda dengan survei yang collect standardized responses dari large samples, wawancara allow evaluators untuk engage dalam dialog interaktif yang dapat menggali nuances, mengeksplorasi complexities, dan uncover insights yang tidak terantisipasi. Dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, wawancara particularly valuable untuk understanding context penggunaan sistem, identifying underlying reasons untuk masalah atau successes, exploring workarounds yang dikembangkan users, dan gathering rich narratives tentang bagaimana sistem impacts work processes dan organizational dynamics.

Wawancara exist pada spectrum dari highly structured hingga completely unstructured. Wawancara terstruktur menggunakan standardized set pertanyaan yang diajukan dalam order yang sama kepada all participants, providing consistency dan facilitating comparison across responses. Wawancara semi-terstruktur menggunakan interview guide dengan core questions tetapi allows flexibility untuk probing, follow-up questions, dan exploration of emerging themes. Wawancara tidak terstruktur bersifat conversational dan exploratory, dengan minimal predetermined structure. Untuk evaluasi sistem informasi, semi-structured interviews often provide optimal balance antara consistency dan flexibility, ensuring coverage of key evaluation questions sambil allowing depth exploration.

### 3.2.1 Perencanaan Wawancara Terstruktur

Perencanaan wawancara yang efektif dimulai dengan clear identification of information needs dan research questions yang harus dijawab melalui wawancara. Evaluator harus explicitly articulate apa yang ingin diketahui, mengapa wawancara adalah metode appropriate (as opposed to atau in addition to surveys atau observations), dan bagaimana interview data akan diintegrasikan dengan data dari metode lain. Misalnya, dalam evaluasi sistem customer relationship management (CRM), wawancara mungkin designed untuk understand how sales staff actually use sistem dalam daily workflow, what challenges mereka encounter, dan how sistem has changed their customer interaction patterns—questions yang require nuanced responses beyond what surveys can capture.

Selection of interview participants critical untuk obtaining relevant dan diverse perspectives. Purposive sampling strategies ensure inclusion dari key stakeholder groups yang memiliki important experiences atau insights tentang sistem. Maximum variation sampling seeks diversity across relevant dimensions—misalnya, including users dengan varying levels of experience, dari different departments, dengan different roles (end users, power users, administrators). Extreme case sampling focuses pada users dengan unusually positive atau negative experiences, yang can illuminate conditions yang contribute to success atau failure. Snowball sampling meminta participants untuk recommend others dengan relevant knowledge, useful untuk identifying hard-to-reach informants.

Sample size untuk wawancara ditentukan oleh principle of saturation—continuing interviews until no substantial new information emerges. Dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, saturation typically reached dengan 15-30 interviews, tergantung pada diversity dari user population dan complexity dari sistem. Practical constraints seperti time dan resources juga influence sample size, tetapi minimal number necessary untuk capturing key perspectives harus ensured. Documentation dari sampling rationale dan achieved sample characteristics important untuk assessing trustworthiness dan transferability of findings.

Logistical planning mencakup scheduling interviews pada times convenient untuk participants, selecting appropriate venues (private space free dari interruptions untuk face-to-face interviews, atau quiet environment untuk phone/video interviews), estimating realistic duration (typically 45-90 minutes untuk in-depth interviews), dan preparing necessary materials (interview guide, consent forms, recording devices, backup batteries). Informed consent process harus explain purpose evaluasi, how interview data akan used, confidentiality protections, voluntary nature of participation, dan right untuk withdraw atau skip questions.

Dalam konteks evaluasi sistem e-procurement di lembaga pemerintah Indonesia, perencanaan wawancara mungkin involve selecting participants from berbagai eselon (staff operasional yang daily users, middle managers yang supervise processes, dan senior officials yang make strategic decisions), dari different departments (procurement, finance, planning), dan dengan varying technical literacy. Considerations tentang hierarchical culture mungkin influence interview approach—misalnya, conducting separate sessions untuk different levels untuk encourage candid responses, atau ensuring appropriate communication protocols when interviewing senior officials.

### 3.2.2 Pengembangan Protokol Wawancara

Protokol wawancara atau interview guide adalah structured framework yang outlines topics to be covered, key questions to be asked, dan potential probes atau follow-up questions. Well-developed protocol ensures consistency across interviews (particularly important ketika multiple interviewers involved), comprehensive coverage of evaluation questions, dan appropriate sequencing of topics. Protocol should be flexible enough untuk accommodate unique circumstances atau emerging insights dari each interview, bukan rigid script yang must be followed verbatim.

Interview protocol typically dimulai dengan opening atau warm-up questions yang easy to answer, non-threatening, dan build rapport—misalnya, asking about participant's role, berapa lama they have been using sistem, atau typical activities mereka involve sistem. Main body of interview explores substantive evaluation questions, organized thematically—misalnya, sections on system features dan functionality, ease of use, impact pada work processes, training dan support, dan suggestions untuk improvement. Closing section provides opportunity untuk participants untuk add any additional thoughts, ask questions about evaluasi, atau provide feedback tentang interview process.

Question formulation requires careful attention untuk clarity, neutrality, dan appropriateness. Open-ended questions encourage detailed responses: "Can you walk me through how you use sistem untuk process purchase orders?" atau "What challenges have you encountered when using sistem?" Questions harus neutral, avoiding leading atau loaded language yang suggests expected responses: instead of "Don't you find sistem difficult to use?", ask "How would you describe your experience learning to use sistem?" Questions harus pitched at appropriate level of abstraction—not too vague ("What do you think about sistem?") atau too specific (unless intentionally probing particular detail).

Probing techniques help evaluators elicit elaboration, clarification, atau examples. Elaboration probes encourage participants untuk expand: "Can you tell me more about that?" atau "What happened next?" Clarification probes ensure understanding: "What do you mean by 'complicated'?" atau "Can you give me an example?" Silent probes—simply pausing dan waiting—often prompt participants untuk continue. Reflection probes repeat atau rephrase what participant said untuk confirm understanding: "So, if I understand correctly, you're saying that..." Probes harus used judiciously untuk maintain conversation flow dan avoid making participant feel interrogated.

Protocol should include prompts untuk handling challenging situations: what to do if participant provides very brief responses (prepared probes untuk encourage elaboration), if participant goes off-topic (gentle redirection strategies), if sensitive or emotional topics arise (appropriate empathetic responses dan reminders tentang confidentiality), atau if participant discloses information yang suggests serious system issues atau risks (procedures untuk documenting dan escalating concerns).

Dalam evaluasi sistem informasi akademik (SIAKAD) di universitas, interview protocol mungkin include sections pada: daily usage patterns ("Describe typical activities Anda lakukan dengan SIAKAD"), specific features ("How do you use grade entry module? What works well? What's frustrating?"), comparison dengan previous systems atau manual processes ("How has SIAKAD changed way Anda manage student records?"), support dan training needs ("What training did you receive? Was it adequate?"), dan future needs ("What features atau improvements would make SIAKAD more useful untuk Anda?"). Protocol mungkin include specific probes untuk technical issues, integration dengan other systems, atau mobile access—topics identified as potentially important dalam preliminary data gathering.

### 3.2.3 Teknik Pelaksanaan Wawancara

Pelaksanaan wawancara yang efektif requires interpersonal skills, active listening, dan adaptive facilitation. Opening wawancara sets tone: interviewer should clearly introduce themselves, explain purpose dan process, review consent dan confidentiality, estimate duration, dan ask if participant has any questions sebelum beginning. Creating comfortable atmosphere—friendly demeanor, appropriate eye contact, interested body language—encourages openness dan trust. Interviewer should be mindful of power dynamics, particularly dalam organizational contexts di mana participants mungkin concerned tentang consequences dari candid responses.

Active listening adalah core skill: interviewer harus genuinely attend to what participant says, demonstrate interest dan understanding melalui verbal affirmations ("I see", "That makes sense") dan nonverbal cues (nodding, appropriate facial expressions), dan resist temptation untuk interrupt atau impose own interpretations. Taking brief notes during interview dapat help track key points dan topics for follow-up, tetapi excessive note-taking dapat disrupt flow dan make participant self-conscious. Audio recording (dengan permission) allows interviewer untuk focus on conversation rather than documentation, tetapi some participants may be inhibited by recording—decision should be made case-by-case dengan appropriate safeguards.

Probing effectively requires judgment about when deeper exploration needed. Not every response requires probing; interviewer must discern which topics warrant elaboration berdasarkan relevance to evaluation questions dan richness of emerging insights. Probes should be organic extension of conversation, bukan disruptive interrogation. Interviewer should allow participants sufficient time untuk think dan formulate responses—comfortable dengan silence rather than rushing to fill pauses. Balance harus maintained between covering protocol topics (ensuring comprehensiveness) dan following interesting tangents yang may yield unexpected insights (allowing emergence).

Managing challenging interview dynamics requires skills dan strategies. For reticent participants who provide minimal responses, interviewer can use specific probes ("Can you give me a specific example of when that happened?"), reassurance tentang value dari their perspectives, atau contextual prompts ("Many users have mentioned X—has that been your experience?"). For verbose participants who provide excessive detail atau go off-topic, interviewer can use gentle redirection ("That's interesting—let me bring us back to...") atau structured transitions ("We've covered X well—I'd like to move to discussing Y"). For emotional responses—frustration with sistem, anxiety about changes—interviewer should respond with empathy dan support sambil maintaining professional boundaries.

Cultural considerations particularly important dalam Indonesian organizational contexts. Respect for hierarchy may influence how participants respond, particularly when discussing senior management decisions atau system implementations. Use of formal language (Anda rather than kamu) appropriate unless participant signals otherwise. Indirect communication styles may mean that criticisms atau problems expressed subtly—interviewer must be attuned to nuances. Building rapport may require more extensive preliminary conversation tentang non-work topics in some cultural contexts. Flexibility tentang interview scheduling untuk accommodate prayer times atau other cultural practices demonstrates respect dan facilitates participation.

Virtual interviews—via video conferencing (Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet) atau phone—increasingly common dan offer advantages in terms of accessibility dan efficiency, tetapi present unique challenges. Technical issues (poor connection, audio problems) dapat disrupt flow; having backup plan (switching to phone) advisable. Visual cues limited atau absent dalam phone interviews; verbal affirmations dan probes become more important. Screen fatigue consideration untuk lengthy virtual interviews; shorter duration atau breaks may be necessary. Recording virtual interviews (dengan permission) often easier technically than face-to-face, providing high-quality audio untuk transcription.

### 3.2.4 Dokumentasi dan Transkripsi Hasil

Comprehensive dan systematic documentation of interview data essential untuk rigorous analysis. Immediately after interview, interviewer should review dan expand field notes sementara details still fresh, documenting not only what was said tetapi also contextual observations (participant's demeanor, environmental factors, notable nonverbal cues). Reflective memo writing—capturing interviewer's initial impressions, emerging themes, methodological notes, atau questions for follow-up—important component of documentation yang facilitates iterative analysis.

Audio transcription converts recorded interviews into text yang can be systematically analyzed. Verbatim transcription includes every word spoken, including false starts, repetitions, dan filler words ("um", "uh"), providing complete record tetapi resulting dalam lengthy texts. Intelligent verbatim transcription removes filler words dan verbal tics sambil preserving all substantive content, creating more readable texts yang retain meaning. Transcription decisions should be consistent across interviews dan documented. For interviews conducted dalam Bahasa Indonesia dengan code-switching to English (common dalam technical discussions), transcription should preserve language mixing untuk capture nuances of how participants express concepts.

Transcription dapat dilakukan manually oleh researcher (time-intensive tetapi allows deep familiarity dengan data), oleh professional transcriptionists (faster tetapi requires quality checking by researcher), atau using automated transcription software (tools seperti Otter.ai, Rev, atau Google's speech-to-text increasingly sophisticated tetapi still require human review dan correction, particularly untuk specialized terminology atau non-standard accents). Regardless of method, transcript accuracy critical; researchers should verify transcripts against recordings, making corrections dan noting uncertainties (inaudible segments marked clearly).

Transcript formatting should facilitate analysis. Speaker identification (labels seperti "Interviewer:" dan "Participant:" atau participant codes untuk anonymity), line numbering (enabling precise citation dalam analysis), timestamps untuk key segments, dan consistent paragraph breaks aid readability dan analysis. Annotations can be added untuk important nonverbal behaviors atau contextual notes ([participant laughs], [long pause], [phone rings]) yang add meaning to verbal content. Anonymization or pseudonymization applied during transcription untuk protect confidentiality—replacing names, specific locations, atau other identifying information.

Data management and security protocols ensure ethical handling of interview data. Audio files dan transcripts should be stored securely, dengan access restricted to research team. File naming conventions should be systematic dan meaningful (e.g., "Interview_UserDept_Finance_001_Date") sambil protecting participant identity. Backup copies maintained untuk prevent data loss. Retention dan destruction schedules should be established—typically, data retained untuk duration of project plus reasonable period untuk verification atau follow-up analysis, kemudian securely deleted sesuai dengan informed consent agreements dan organizational data policies.

Dalam evaluasi sistem e-health di rumah sakit, misalnya, interview dengan doctors dan nurses tentang clinical information system recorded dan transcribed verbatim. Transcripts diformat with speaker identification, line numbers, dan timestamps. Technical medical terms verified untuk accuracy. Patient names atau case details mentioned dalam interviews replaced dengan pseudonyms dalam transcripts untuk maintain confidentiality. Transcripts kemudian imported into qualitative data analysis software (NVivo, ATLAS.ti, atau MAXQDA) untuk systematic coding dan analysis.

### 3.2.5 Analisis Data Wawancara

Analisis data wawancara adalah iterative dan interpretive process yang transforms raw interview data into meaningful findings dan insights. Thematic analysis—identifying, analyzing, dan reporting patterns (themes) within data—adalah approach paling common dalam evaluasi sistem informasi. Process begins dengan familiarization: researchers immerse themselves dalam data by reading dan re-reading transcripts, listening to recordings, dan reviewing notes, developing holistic sense of content dan starting to notice patterns.

Initial coding involves systematically labeling segments of data dengan descriptive codes yang capture essence of content. Codes can be data-driven (inductive)—emerging directly from what participants say—atau concept-driven (deductive)—derived from evaluation framework, theory, atau a priori interests. For example, dalam evaluasi sistem ERP, inductive codes might include "workarounds" (practices developed untuk circumvent system limitations) atau "informal training" (learning from colleagues), sementara deductive codes might include predefined categories seperti "system quality", "information quality", "service quality" dari established models.

Coding can be done manually (using highlighters on printed transcripts atau comments dalam word processors) atau using Computer-Assisted Qualitative Data Analysis Software (CAQDAS) seperti NVivo, ATLAS.ti, MAXQDA, atau Dedoose. These tools facilitate organization, retrieval, dan visualization of coded data; enable queries dan comparisons (e.g., comparing codes across different user groups); dan provide audit trails of analytical decisions. However, software doesn't do analysis—researcher's interpretive judgment remains central.

From initial codes, researchers develop higher-level themes by grouping related codes, identifying patterns, dan constructing coherent narratives. Themes should be more abstract dan interpretive than codes, capturing underlying meanings atau patterns across data. For instance, codes "workarounds", "informal training", "local customizations" might be grouped under theme "user adaptation strategies" yang reflects how users proactively modify their practices atau system configurations untuk better fit needs. Theme development iterative: researchers refine themes, merge similar ones, split complex ones, atau discard those weakly supported by data.

Themes are then reviewed dan refined through iterative process of checking against coded extracts (does theme accurately represent coded data?) dan entire dataset (does theme pattern hold across all interviews?). Relationships between themes explored—are some themes subordinate to others? Do themes represent sequential processes atau different dimensions of phenomenon? Thematic map atau network diagrams can visualize relationships. Final stage involves defining dan naming themes clearly, articulating what each captures, dan writing rich descriptions illustrated dengan compelling data excerpts.

Alternative analytical approaches include framework analysis (matrix-based method particularly useful untuk policy-relevant evaluasi dengan specific questions), grounded theory (building theoretical models dari data), atau narrative analysis (examining how participants construct stories tentang their experiences). Choice of approach depends on evaluation questions, theoretical orientation, dan nature of data. Regardless of approach, analysis should be systematic, transparent, dan reflexive—acknowledging researcher's role dalam shaping interpretations.

Quality of analysis assessed through trustworthiness criteria. Credibility enhanced through prolonged engagement dengan data, triangulation dengan other data sources, member checking (asking participants untuk validate interpretations), dan peer debriefing. Transferability supported by thick description of context, participants, dan processes, enabling readers untuk judge applicability to their contexts. Dependability ensured through audit trail documenting analytical decisions. Confirmability demonstrated through grounding interpretations dalam data dengan extensive quotations dan clear linkages between data dan conclusions.

Dalam evaluasi sistem supply chain management di perusahaan retail, analisis wawancara dengan warehouse managers, logistics coordinators, dan procurement staff mungkin reveal themes seperti: (1) "integration challenges"—difficulties dalam data synchronization antara sistem baru dengan legacy systems; (2) "mobile necessity"—critical need untuk mobile access untuk users yang constantly moving dalam warehouse; (3) "trust through visibility"—increased confidence dalam inventory data karena real-time tracking; (4) "training gap"—inadequate preparation untuk advanced features leading to underutilization. These themes, illustrated dengan participant quotations dan interpreted dalam context of organizational goals, provide nuanced understanding beyond what quantitative metrics alone reveal, informing targeted improvement initiatives.

## 3.3 Observasi Partisipatif

Observasi partisipatif adalah metode pengumpulan data di mana evaluator secara langsung mengamati fenomena yang dievaluasi dalam setting natural mereka, seringkali sambil berpartisipasi dalam activities yang diamati. Dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, observasi allows evaluators untuk witness firsthand bagaimana sistem actually digunakan dalam daily work contexts, mengidentifikasi discrepancies antara intended use dan actual practices, dan memahami contextual factors yang influence system effectiveness. Observasi particularly valuable untuk capturing tacit knowledge, informal workarounds, dan socio-technical interactions yang users may tidak fully aware of atau unable to articulate dalam surveys atau interviews.

Observasi dapat vary dalam level of participation, dari complete participant (evaluator fully engage dalam activities sebagai member of group) hingga complete observer (evaluator observe tanpa interaction). Dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, observer-as-participant stance paling common—evaluator's role sebagai observer adalah known, tetapi mereka may engage dalam some activities atau interactions untuk build rapport dan gain deeper understanding. Misalnya, evaluator mungkin spend time dalam customer service department, observing bagaimana staff use CRM system untuk handle customer inquiries, occasionally asking questions untuk clarify observations tetapi not performing customer service tasks themselves.

### 3.3.1 Konsep Observasi Partisipatif

Observasi partisipatif berakar dalam tradisi etnografi antropologi tetapi telah diadaptasi widely untuk organizational dan technology studies. Core principle adalah bahwa understanding human behavior dan social phenomena requires studying them dalam natural contexts di mana they occur, rather than dalam artificial atau controlled settings. Dalam konteks sistem informasi, natural contexts include offices di mana users bekerja, meeting rooms di mana decisions dibuat, help desks di mana support provided, atau mobile environments di mana field workers operate.

Key premise adalah bahwa much of what influences system use adalah embedded dalam social practices, organizational routines, dan physical environments yang cannot be fully captured through self-reports. Users may have developed workarounds yang they consider normal dan tidak mention dalam interviews. Organizational culture may influence adoption patterns dalam ways yang participants cannot articulate. Physical workspace layout may facilitate atau hinder collaboration around shared systems. Observasi enables discovery of these contextual factors.

Observasi partisipatif distinguished dari simple observation by researcher's engagement dengan participants dan setting. Participation serves multiple purposes: building trust dan rapport yang encourage openness, gaining insider perspective on activities dan meanings, developing empathy dan deeper understanding of user experiences, dan creating opportunities untuk informal conversations yang complement formal interviews. Level of participation carefully negotiated untuk balance benefits of engagement against risks of observer bias atau disrupting natural behaviors.

Dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, observasi particularly useful untuk understanding: actual workflows dan how sistem fits (atau doesn't fit) into them; communication patterns among users dan between users dan technical support; problem-solving strategies when sistem encounters issues; differences between official procedures dan actual practices; environmental factors affecting system use (noise levels, lighting, workspace configurations); dan temporal patterns (peak usage times, seasonal variations, emergency situations).

Observasi dapat standalone method atau combined dengan others dalam mixed-methods design. Sequential design mungkin use observasi untuk exploratory phase, identifying issues untuk subsequent investigation melalui surveys atau interviews. Concurrent design mungkin use observasi alongside interviews, dengan observasi providing contextual grounding untuk interpreting interview data. Observasi data can validate atau challenge findings dari other methods—for instance, usage patterns observed may differ dari self-reported behaviors dalam surveys.

### 3.3.2 Peran Observer dalam Evaluasi Sistem

Peran observer dalam evaluasi sistem harus carefully defined dan negotiated dengan organizational stakeholders. Clear definition of role includes: what evaluator will observe (specific activities, settings, times), what level of participation involved, how evaluator will introduce themselves to participants, what access permissions required, dan how observations will be documented dan used. Formal agreement atau protocol dengan organizational leadership establishes legitimacy dan facilitates access, sementara individual consent dari those being observed ensures ethical practice.

Observer's stance exists pada continuum. Complete participant stance—di mana evaluator's identity sebagai researcher hidden dan they fully participate as organizational member—rarely appropriate dalam evaluasi contexts karena ethical concerns tentang deception. Participant-as-observer stance involves known researcher role dengan active participation dalam some activities—for instance, evaluator might attend team meetings, engage dalam informal conversations, atau try using sistem themselves untuk understand user experience. Observer-as-participant involves known observer role dengan minimal participation—mainly watching dan taking notes, dengan occasional questions. Complete observer stance involves no interaction—for instance, observing user interactions dengan sistem melalui security cameras atau screen recordings, raising significant ethical issues about consent dan privacy.

In practice, observers often shift along this continuum based on situations. During formal meetings, complete observer stance may be appropriate. During work activities, observer-as-participant stance allows observation sambil avoiding disruption. During breaks atau informal settings, participant-as-observer stance facilitates relationship building. Flexibility dalam role adaptation enables observers untuk respond appropriately to social dynamics sambil maintaining clarity tentang primary purpose: evaluation.

Building trust essential untuk effective observation. Observers should be transparent tentang purpose, respectful of participants' time dan privacy, non-judgmental dalam demeanor, dan maintain confidentiality. Spending time dalam setting sebelum intensive observation ("hanging around") helps participants become comfortable dengan observer presence, reducing reactivity—change dalam behaviors because people know they're being observed. Sharing preliminary observations atau validating interpretations dengan participants demonstrates respect for their expertise dan insights.

Observers must be reflexive tentang their impact on settings. Hawthorne effect—change dalam behaviors when people know they're observed—unavoidable tetapi can be minimized through prolonged presence (behaviors tend to normalize over time) dan unobtrusive positioning. Observer bias—tendency untuk see what one expects atau wants to see—mitigated through structured observation protocols, multiple observers (when feasible), dan systematic recording. Role conflicts may arise when observers witness problematic practices—balancing obligations to accurately document observations dengan relationships dengan participants requires careful ethical navigation.

Dalam evaluasi implementasi sistem digital signature di institusi perbankan, observer's role mungkin negotiated sebagai: attending training sessions (as complete observer untuk minimize disruption), observing tellers processing customer transactions (as observer-as-participant, occasionally asking clarifying questions during lulls), participating dalam team meetings about implementation challenges (as participant-as-observer), dan trying out sistem dalam test environment (as complete participant untuk gain user perspective). Each setting requires appropriate role adaptation sambil maintaining transparent researcher identity.

### 3.3.3 Teknik Observasi dan Pencatatan Data

Systematic observation requires clear focus on what to observe, structured methods untuk recording observations, dan disciplined practices untuk ensuring comprehensiveness dan accuracy. Observation guide atau protocol specifies dimensions atau aspects to attend to, providing framework sambil allowing flexibility untuk unexpected phenomena. For system evaluation, observation dimensions might include: physical setting (workspace layout, equipment, environmental conditions), participants (roles, characteristics, interactions), activities (tasks performed, sequences, durations), artifacts (documents, screens, tools used), events (meetings, training sessions, incidents), time (temporal patterns, sequences), dan goals (purposes of activities, outcomes sought).

Field notes are primary documentation method—detailed descriptions written during atau immediately after observations. Descriptive field notes capture concrete details: what observer sees, hears, dan experiences, using specific, sensory language. Example: "User switches between three different screens to compile information for one report, jotting numbers on sticky notes as intermediate step. When system freezes during save operation, user sighs, waits approximately 30 seconds, then clicks 'X' to force close dan restart process." Reflective field notes capture observer's interpretations, emerging insights, methodological notes, atau personal reactions. Example: "User's workaround with sticky notes suggests system doesn't support easy cross-referencing. Freezing incident might be network issue—observe during peak hours to check if pattern."

Field notes should be chronologically organized, dengan timestamps untuk reference. Headings atau section markers help organize notes by settings atau activities. Sketches atau diagrams supplement textual descriptions—for instance, mapping physical layout of office showing screen visibility lines, atau diagramming workflow steps. Photographs (dengan permission) provide visual documentation of settings, artifacts, atau practices. Audio atau video recordings capture richness of interactions tetapi require careful ethical consideration dan may heighten reactivity.

Different recording strategies suit different situations. Concurrent recording—writing notes during observation—provides detailed, immediate documentation tetapi can be obtrusive atau may miss fast-moving events. Post-observation recording—writing detailed notes immediately after observation—less obtrusive tetapi relies on memory dan may lose detail. Jotting—brief notes atau keywords during observation, expanded into full notes afterward—compromise strategy. Choice depends on setting norms (is note-taking during meetings acceptable?), observer's capacity untuk multitask, dan nature of activities observed.

Sampling strategies determine when dan where to observe. Time sampling involves observing at predetermined intervals atau times—for instance, observing help desk at different hours untuk capture variation dalam load dan types of issues. Event sampling focuses on specific types of events—for instance, observing user onboarding sessions, system update deployments, atau recovery from system failures. Setting sampling involves observing dalam different locations—different departments, offices, atau physical environments di mana sistem used. Comprehensive observation strategy typically combines approaches untuk capture range of conditions dan contexts.

Technology-augmented observation methods increasingly available. Screen recording software captures actual system usage patterns—clicks, navigation paths, time spent on tasks—providing objective behavioral data. Eye-tracking devices reveal what parts of interface users attend to. Usability testing dalam labs uses think-aloud protocols di mana users verbalize thoughts saat interacting dengan system, combined dengan observation of behaviors. Wearable sensors atau mobile apps can track movements atau environmental conditions untuk field workers using mobile systems. These methods should complement rather than replace direct human observation, which captures social dan contextual dimensions.

Dalam observasi penggunaan sistem learning management di sekolah, observer might document: physical setting (classroom layout, technology available, internet connectivity quality), teacher activities (how they navigate sistem, what features used, any difficulties encountered), student behaviors (engagement levels, assistance seeking, off-task technology use), interactions (teacher-student help with system, peer collaboration), temporal patterns (time spent on different activities), dan incidents (login problems, content not loading, confusion tentang assignment submission). Combination of concurrent jotting, post-observation detailed write-ups, screen recordings (dengan permission), dan photographs of classroom technology setup provides comprehensive documentation.

### 3.3.4 Etika Observasi Partisipatif

Ethical observation practices essential untuk protecting participants dan maintaining integrity of evaluation. Informed consent foundational—participants should understand that they're being observed, for what purposes, how data will be used, what confidentiality protections provided, dan their rights (including right to decline atau withdraw). In organizational settings, multiple levels of consent may be required: organizational leadership approval for access, supervisors' permission untuk observe their teams, dan individual consent dari those directly observed. Consent should be informed (participants understand what they're agreeing to), voluntary (no coercion atau undue pressure), dan documented.

Obtaining meaningful consent dalam observational contexts challenging. Group situations complicate individual consent—when observing team meetings atau shared workspaces, ensuring all present have consented requires coordination. Implied consent approaches—where notices about ongoing observation posted dan continuing to be present constitutes consent—may be practical tetapi less robust than explicit consent. Ongoing consent important dalam extended observations—checking in periodically dengan participants to reaffirm comfort dengan observation.

Confidentiality dan anonymity protections crucial. Participants' identities should be protected dalam reports unless they explicitly consent to being identified dan there's good reason to do so. Descriptive details in field notes atau reports should be aggregated atau altered to prevent identification—for instance, referring to "senior manager" rather than naming specific individual. Sensitive information observed—such as personal conflicts, performance issues, atau system misuse—should be handled dengan care, balancing accurate documentation untuk evaluation with protection of individuals' reputations dan privacy.

Covert observation—where participants unaware they're being observed atau observer's identity concealed—generally ethically problematic. Deception undermines trust, violates autonomy, dan can cause harm if participants later discover they were unknowingly studied. Exceptions might be justified dalam rare cases where overt observation would fundamentally change behaviors dalam ways yang defeat evaluation purposes, significant public interest served, no other feasible methods exist, dan procedures untuk eventual disclosure dan debriefing implemented. However, such exceptions should be rare dan carefully reviewed by ethics committees.

Observers may witness problematic atau unethical practices—system misuse, security violations, discrimination, atau harassment. Ethical responsibilities include: accurately documenting what observed (without exaggeration atau omission), protecting whistle-blowers atau vulnerable individuals who may be implicated, reporting serious violations to appropriate authorities (particularly legally mandated reports tentang safety atau abuse), dan separating evaluation findings dari individual accountability issues. Predetermined protocols untuk handling sensitive observations help observers navigate these situations.

Power dynamics require attention. Observers typically have more power than participants in organizational hierarchies—they report to leadership, their findings may influence decisions affecting participants. This asymmetry can create anxiety atau influence behaviors. Observers should be sensitive to these dynamics, avoid exploiting trust, transparently communicate how findings will be used, dan advocate for participants' interests when appropriate. In some cultural contexts, presence of outsider observer may particularly intimidate—extra efforts to build rapport dan demonstrate trustworthiness necessary.

Dalam observasi implementasi sistem elektronik medical records di klinik, etika considerations include: obtaining institutional review board approval; informed consent dari healthcare providers being observed; patient consent when observations occur during clinical encounters; protecting patient confidentiality dalam field notes (no patient names atau identifying information); handling observations of potential medical errors atau safety issues (clear protocols untuk reporting); being sensitive to power dynamics between physicians, nurses, dan support staff; dan minimizing disruption to clinical care. Observers must balance comprehensiveness of data collection dengan primacy of patient care dan privacy.

### 3.3.5 Analisis Data Observasi

Analisis data observasi transforms descriptive field notes into analytical insights dan findings. Process begins dengan organizing dan managing observational data—chronologically ordered field notes indexed by date, setting, participants, dan activities; photographs catalogued dengan captions; audio atau video recordings transcribed atau summarized; dan all materials securely stored. Data inventory atau log helps researchers track corpus of observational data dan ensures nothing overlooked.

Immersion dan familiarization phase involves reading dan re-reading field notes, viewing images atau recordings, dan writing analytic memos tentang emerging impressions. Researchers begin identifying patterns, surprises, contradictions, atau themes requiring deeper investigation. Chronological reading provides sense of how phenomena develop over time, while thematic reading (grouping notes by topics atau settings) reveals patterns across contexts.

Coding observational data similar to coding interview transcripts—systematically labeling segments dengan descriptive codes. However, observational data adds layers: descriptive codes capture what happened (activities, interactions, events); interpretive codes capture meanings atau significance; pattern codes identify relationships atau recurring themes. For instance, observation of users repeatedly checking multiple systems untuk same information might be coded descriptively as "cross-system verification", interpretively as "lack of trust dalam data integrity", dan at pattern level as "integration deficiency impact".

Analytic strategies dapat include: Domain analysis—identifying semantic relationships among categories (e.g., types of workarounds, kinds of training, forms of resistance). Temporal analysis—examining sequences, phases, atau cycles (e.g., how system adoption unfolds over time). Spatial analysis—exploring how physical environments shape practices (e.g., how workspace layout affects collaboration around shared screens). Comparative analysis—contrasting observations across settings, times, atau groups (e.g., differences dalam system use between departments). Event analysis—focusing on critical incidents atau typical episodes (e.g., anatomy of successful vs. problematic transactions).

Pattern matching involves linking observational patterns to theoretical concepts atau evaluation frameworks. For example, observations of users developing elaborate workarounds might be interpreted through lens of technology acceptance models (suggesting usability atau usefulness issues), socio-technical systems theory (revealing misalignment between technology dan work practices), atau organizational learning perspectives (demonstrating adaptive innovation). Theoretical sensitivity helps researchers recognize significance of patterns.

Triangulation dengan other data sources strengthens analysis. Observational findings can be compared dengan: interview accounts (do observed practices align dengan how users describe them?), survey responses (do behavioral patterns match reported attitudes?), system logs (do observed usage patterns correspond with digital traces?), atau organizational documents (are official procedures reflected dalam actual practices?). Convergence increases confidence; divergence signals need untuk deeper investigation of reasons untuk discrepancies.

Negative case analysis—actively searching untuk instances yang contradict emerging patterns—important quality check. If analysis suggests users generally avoid advanced features, researcher should specifically look untuk users who do use them dan understand what's different. Negative cases refine understanding by revealing boundary conditions atau moderating factors.

Findings presented through thick description—rich, detailed accounts yang provide readers dengan vicarious experience of observed settings dan sufficient context untuk judge transferability. Analytical narrative weaves together descriptive excerpts dari field notes, interpretive commentary, theoretical connections, dan implications untuk system design atau improvement. Visual representations—process diagrams showing observed workflows, spatial maps showing movement patterns, matrices comparing practices across groups—complement textual analysis.

Dalam analisis observasi penggunaan sistem inventory management di pabrik, researchers might identify patterns seperti: "dual-system operation" (workers maintaining parallel paper records karena distrust dalam digital system's reliability), "selective digitalization" (only entering data required untuk compliance, keeping detailed notes offline), "hierarchical data flow" (information passing through supervisors rather than entered directly by floor workers), dan "peak-time abandonment" (reverting to manual processes during high-production periods when system slowness problematic). These patterns, grounded dalam specific field note examples dan contextualized within production realities, reveal socio-technical dynamics yang quantitative performance metrics alone wouldn't expose, informing redesign priorities yang address actual work practices.

## 3.4 Analisis Log Sistem

Analisis log sistem adalah metode pengumpulan data yang memanfaatkan digital traces—automatically recorded data tentang system activities, user interactions, performance metrics, dan events—untuk evaluasi berbasis bukti objektif. Dalam era digital di mana hampir semua sistem informasi generates extensive logs, metode ini provides unprecedented access to fine-grained behavioral data dan system performance indicators yang complement self-reported data dari surveys atau interviews. Log analysis particularly powerful untuk understanding actual usage patterns (berbeda dari intended atau reported usage), identifying performance bottlenecks, detecting security anomalies, dan providing quantitative evidence untuk decision-making.

Berbeda dengan metode yang rely on human perception atau memory, system logs provide objective, precise, dan comprehensive records of what actually transpired. Logs capture every transaction, click, error, dan system event dengan timestamp accuracy, enabling detailed reconstruction of sequences, patterns, dan anomalies. Scale dan granularity data yang dapat dikumpulkan melalui logs far exceeds what feasible through manual observation atau surveys. However, logs juga present challenges: massive volume requires sophisticated tools dan skills untuk processing, raw log data often cryptic dan requires domain knowledge untuk interpretation, dan logs capture behaviors but not intentions atau contextual meanings, necessitating integration dengan qualitative methods.

### 3.4.1 Jenis-jenis Log Sistem

Sistem informasi modern generates berbagai types of logs, each capturing different aspects of system behavior dan serving different analytical purposes. Access logs record user authentication events—login attempts (successful dan failed), session duration, logout events, dan access to specific resources atau modules. Analysis of access logs reveals usage patterns (who uses system, when, how often), identifies dormant accounts yang may represent security risks, detects unusual access patterns yang may indicate account compromise, dan provides data untuk capacity planning.

Transaction logs atau application logs record business transactions dan user interactions within system—what actions performed, on what data, at what time, dan with what results. For example, dalam ERP system, transaction logs capture purchase orders created, invoices processed, inventory movements, dan report generations. Analysis reveals which features actively used versus ignored, identifies frequently performed tasks yang may benefit dari streamlining, exposes error-prone workflows, dan enables process mining—reconstructing actual business processes dari event logs untuk comparison dengan designed processes.

Error logs document system malfunctions, exceptions, warnings, dan failures—application errors, database errors, failed transactions, timeouts, atau resource exhaustion. Error analysis critical untuk identifying reliability issues, understanding user pain points (frequent errors dalam certain modules), prioritizing bug fixes, dan detecting patterns yang may indicate underlying infrastructure problems. Severity classification dalam error logs (critical, major, minor) helps prioritize responses.

Performance logs capture system resource utilization dan response metrics—CPU usage, memory consumption, disk I/O, network bandwidth, database query times, page load times, atau transaction throughput. Performance analysis identifies bottlenecks, supports capacity planning, validates whether system meets service level agreements (SLAs), dan reveals degradation patterns yang may require optimization atau infrastructure upgrades. Time-series analysis of performance logs can detect trends dan predict future capacity needs.

Security logs atau audit logs record security-relevant events—authentication attempts, authorization checks, privilege escalations, configuration changes, data access to sensitive information, atau policy violations. Security analysis detects intrusion attempts, identifies compromised accounts, ensures compliance dengan audit requirements, dan reconstructs security incidents untuk forensic investigation. Regulatory frameworks (seperti GDPR, HIPAA, atau local data protection laws) often mandate specific audit logging.

Network logs capture communication patterns—HTTP requests, database queries, API calls, message queues, atau inter-service communications dalam distributed systems. Network analysis reveals integration points, detects communication failures, identifies chatty interfaces yang may cause performance issues, dan maps actual system architecture (which may differ dari documentation). Dalam cloud atau microservices environments, distributed tracing logs correlate events across multiple services untuk end-to-end transaction visibility.

Dalam evaluasi sistem e-commerce, berbagai log types provide complementary insights: access logs show customer traffic patterns dan peak usage times; transaction logs reveal product browsing behaviors, shopping cart abandonment rates, dan checkout process completion; error logs identify pages atau features dengan high failure rates; performance logs track page load times dan their impact on conversion rates; security logs detect fraud attempts atau account takeovers; dan network logs expose third-party service latencies affecting user experience.

### 3.4.2 Teknik Ekstraksi Data Log

Ekstraksi data dari system logs requires technical skills dan appropriate tools, mengingat logs typically stored dalam diverse formats, distributed across multiple systems, dan characterized by high volume. Log formats vary widely: plain text files (dengan structure yang may be consistent atau highly variable), structured formats (JSON, XML, CSV), database tables, atau binary formats requiring specialized parsers. Understanding format essential untuk designing extraction strategies.

Log aggregation adalah first step untuk distributed systems—collecting logs dari multiple sources (application servers, database servers, web servers, load balancers, network devices) into centralized repository. Tools seperti Fluentd, Logstash (part of ELK stack), atau Splunk Forwarders dapat collect logs dari diverse sources, handle different formats, dan stream them to central storage. Aggregation strategies must consider: real-time versus batch collection (real-time critical untuk monitoring, batch sufficient untuk historical analysis), network bandwidth (logs can be voluminous), data retention (how long to keep raw logs), dan high availability (ensuring log collection doesn't become single point of failure).

Parsing transforms raw log entries into structured data that can be analyzed. Regular expressions (regex) commonly used untuk extracting fields dari semi-structured text logs. For example, parsing Apache web server log line:
```
192.168.1.100 - - [10/Oct/2023:13:55:36 +0700] "GET /products/laptop HTTP/1.1" 200 2326
```
Regex dapat extract: IP address (192.168.1.100), timestamp (10/Oct/2023:13:55:36 +0700), HTTP method (GET), URL (/products/laptop), status code (200), dan bytes transferred (2326). Parsing tools (Logstash Grok patterns, Fluentd parsers, Python's logging libraries) simplify this process dengan predefined patterns untuk common log formats.

Filtering dan sampling manage log volume untuk specific analyses. Filtering selects relevant subset—for instance, extracting only error entries, atau only logs dari production environment, atau only activities by specific user group. Sampling selects representative subset untuk analysis when full dataset too large—systematic sampling (every nth entry), stratified sampling (ensuring representation dari different time periods atau log sources), atau random sampling. Sampling trade-offs: reduces processing requirements tetapi may miss rare but important events.

Data transformation prepares log data untuk analysis. Timestamp normalization converts different time formats to consistent standard dan handles time zones (critical untuk distributed systems). IP address resolution converts numerical IPs to hostnames atau geographic locations. User ID mapping links cryptic system identifiers to meaningful user roles atau departments. Enrichment adds contextual information—for example, augmenting transaction logs dengan product categories, customer segments, atau business unit affiliations dari master data. These transformations make logs more interpretable dan analytically useful.

API-based extraction increasingly important untuk modern systems. Many applications provide APIs untuk programmatic log access—for instance, cloud services (AWS CloudWatch, Google Cloud Logging, Azure Monitor) offer APIs untuk querying logs. RESTful APIs allow scripted extraction, filtering, dan export of log data. Advantages include: structured data formats (typically JSON), fine-grained access control, rate limiting untuk managing load, dan real-time access. Challenges include: API documentation complexity, authentication requirements, pagination untuk large result sets, dan rate limits that may necessitate batching requests.

Dalam large-scale systems, log extraction often automated through data pipelines—scheduled jobs that periodically extract, transform, dan load (ETL) logs into analytical databases atau data warehouses. Tools seperti Apache NiFi, Apache Airflow, atau custom scripts orchestrate these pipelines. Monitoring pipeline health (detecting failures, data quality issues, delays) critical untuk ensuring reliable log analysis.

Untuk evaluasi sistem perpustakaan digital, log extraction strategy might involve: aggregating logs dari web application, OPAC (Online Public Access Catalog), digital repository, dan authentication service; parsing access logs untuk extract user sessions, search queries, dan resource access events; filtering untuk focus on authenticated users (excluding crawlers atau bots); enriching dengan user affiliations (student, faculty, staff) dan resource metadata (book classifications, journal subjects); dan loading structured data into analytics database untuk querying dan visualization.

### 3.4.3 Pembersihan dan Preprocessing Data Log

Raw log data typically contains noise, inconsistencies, dan artifacts that must be cleaned before meaningful analysis possible. Data quality assessment identifies issues: missing values (incomplete log entries), duplicates (same event logged multiple times), outliers (extreme values yang may indicate errors atau significant events), inconsistent formats (especially across different systems atau log sources), dan irrelevant entries (system heartbeats, debugging messages dalam production logs).

Handling missing data depends on nature dan extent. If critical fields missing (like timestamps atau user IDs), affected entries may need to be excluded. If optional fields missing, analysis can proceed dengan awareness of limitations. Imputation—inferring missing values based on available data—possible dalam some contexts (for instance, inferring session end time based on next session start), tetapi assumptions harus clearly documented. Pattern analysis of missing data may itself be informative—for example, consistent missing of certain fields might indicate logging configuration issue.

Deduplication removes redundant log entries that can skew analysis. Exact duplicates (identical entries repeated) can be removed straightforwardly. Near-duplicates (entries nearly identical but dengan minor variations, perhaps from retry attempts) require more sophisticated matching algorithms. Decision harus made whether to completely remove duplicates atau to count them differently—for instance, failed transaction dengan three retry attempts might be counted as one failed transaction atau as signal of persistent issue.

Outlier handling requires domain judgment. Extreme values may represent genuine anomalies that are analytically important—for instance, unusually long transaction time may indicate performance issue, atau spike dalam failed login attempts may indicate security attack. Conversely, outliers may represent data errors—for instance, timestamp in future, atau response time of zero. Statistical methods (like identifying values beyond certain standard deviations dari mean) can flag outliers, but domain experts should decide whether to keep, remove, atau investigate further.

Data type validation ensures fields have correct types dan formats. Timestamps should parse correctly dan fall within plausible ranges. Numerical fields (like response times atau byte counts) should be non-negative. Categorical fields should contain expected values—for instance, HTTP status codes should be valid code numbers. Validation rules expose data quality issues that may require correction at source atau filtering dalam analysis.

Sessionization—grouping individual log entries into user sessions—critical untuk understanding user behaviors. Session typically defined as sequence of activities by same user within certain time window; inactivity beyond threshold (commonly 30 minutes) triggers new session. Sessionization requires: sorting logs by user dan timestamp, identifying session boundaries based on timeout atau explicit logout events, assigning session IDs, dan calculating session-level metrics (duration, page views, actions completed). Challenges include handling concurrent sessions (same user on multiple devices) dan defining appropriate timeout thresholds (may vary by application type).

Bot detection dan filtering important untuk accurate user behavior analysis. Automated crawlers, scrapers, atau monitoring tools generate logs but don't represent human users. Detection methods include: user-agent string analysis (identifying known bot signatures), behavioral patterns (bots typically have higher request rates, more uniform timing, specific navigation patterns), IP blacklists, dan honeypot techniques (links invisible to humans but followed by bots). Depending on analysis goals, bot traffic may be filtered out atau analyzed separately.

Data normalization standardizes representations untuk consistency. URL normalization removes query parameters atau trailing slashes untuk group equivalent accesses. Text normalization handles case sensitivity (converting to lowercase for case-insensitive matching). Time binning groups continuous timestamps into discrete intervals (hourly, daily) untuk temporal analysis. Category mapping converts granular categories into broader groups—for instance, mapping detailed product codes into product categories untuk higher-level analysis.

Dalam preprocessing logs dari sistem online learning platform untuk usage analysis, steps might include: removing entries dengan missing user IDs (cannot attribute to users); deduplicating video progress events (sent every 10 seconds, creating redundancy); sessionizing activities dengan 45-minute timeout (reflecting typical study session); detecting dan filtering LMS maintenance bots using user-agent strings; flagging outliers like video watch duration exceeding video length (suggests playback issues); normalizing course IDs to course names untuk interpretability; dan creating derived variables like "completion rate" (percentage of course materials accessed).

### 3.4.4 Analisis Pola dari Log Sistem

Pattern analysis dalam system logs reveals insights tentang system usage, performance characteristics, dan potential problems. Descriptive analysis provides overview of log data distributions. Frequency analysis counts occurrences—which features most used, most common errors, most accessed resources. Distribution analysis examines variability—spread of response times, distribution of session durations, atau transaction volumes. Summary statistics (means, medians, percentiles) characterize typical behaviors, while variance metrics indicate consistency atau volatility.

Temporal pattern analysis explores how metrics change over time. Time-series analysis tracks metrics at regular intervals—hourly transaction volumes, daily error counts, weekly active users. Trend analysis identifies long-term patterns—is usage growing, declining, atau stable? Seasonal atau cyclical patterns emerge—daily peaks during business hours, weekly lulls on weekends, monthly spikes at reporting deadlines. Anomaly detection identifies deviations dari expected patterns—sudden traffic spike might indicate marketing campaign success (atau DDoS attack), performance degradation might signal infrastructure issues.

Sequence analysis examines ordered patterns dalam user behaviors. Clickstream analysis maps navigation paths through system—which pages visited in what order, where users enter dan exit, common pathways versus rare ones. Process mining reconstructs business processes dari transaction logs—for instance, deriving actual procurement process (request → approval → order → receipt → payment) dan comparing dengan intended process untuk identify deviations, bottlenecks, atau workarounds. Sequential pattern mining discovers frequently occurring sequences—for instance, "users who search for X typically then view Y dan Z."

Cohort analysis compares groups of users atau transactions. User cohorts might be defined by registration date (comparing adoption patterns across cohorts), user roles (comparing usage between departments), atau initial experience (comparing users who received training versus self-taught). Transaction cohorts might be grouped by type, value, atau complexity. Analysis reveals whether behaviors differ significantly across cohorts, informing targeted interventions atau personalization.

Correlation analysis identifies relationships between variables. Do longer session durations correlate dengan more transactions completed? Does error frequency correlate dengan system load? Does time-of-day correlate dengan performance degradation? Correlation doesn't imply causation but highlights relationships worthy of further investigation. Network analysis can map relationships—which users collaborate (based on shared document access), which system components interact frequently (from API call logs), atau which errors co-occur (suggesting related root causes).

Funnel analysis tracks conversion through multi-step processes. E-commerce checkout funnel tracks progression dari cart → shipping → payment → confirmation, identifying steps dengan highest abandonment. Application onboarding funnel tracks new user progression through setup steps. Drop-off rates at each stage reveal friction points. Comparing conversion rates across user segments atau time periods identifies factors affecting success.

Machine learning techniques enable sophisticated pattern discovery. Classification models predict outcomes—for instance, classifying transactions as likely to succeed atau fail based on features extracted dari logs. Clustering algorithms group similar patterns—for instance, clustering user sessions by behavior profiles (researchers, browsers, purchasers). Anomaly detection algorithms (like Isolation Forests, One-Class SVMs) automatically identify unusual patterns warranting investigation—potential security breaches, system malfunctions, atau fraud.

Dalam sistem banking online, log pattern analysis might reveal: peak usage during lunch hours dan evenings (informing capacity planning); strong correlation between mobile app response time dan abandonment rate (prioritizing mobile performance); common sequence: balance check → fund transfer → logout (suggesting primary use case); cohort analysis showing declining engagement after 6 months (triggering retention strategies); anomaly detection flagging unusual nighttime transactions dari dormant accounts (potential fraud); dan funnel analysis revealing 40% drop-off at OTP verification step (indicating usability issue requiring redesign).

### 3.4.5 Visualisasi Data Log

Visualisasi transforms complex log data into interpretable graphical representations that facilitate understanding, communication, dan decision-making. Effective visualizations reveal patterns, highlight anomalies, enable comparisons, dan tell compelling data stories. Choice of visualization depends on data type (categorical, numerical, temporal), analytical purpose (exploration, explanation, monitoring), dan audience (technical teams, management, end users).

Time-series visualizations essential untuk temporal data. Line charts display metrics over time—response times, error rates, user counts—enabling trend identification dan anomaly spotting. Multiple lines can compare different metrics atau segments (different servers, user groups, geographic regions). Area charts emphasize magnitude dan can be stacked untuk showing composition (total traffic split by source). Calendar heatmaps display daily values dalam calendar layout, revealing weekly atau seasonal patterns—darker cells indicate higher values, instantly showing which days experienced peak activity.

Distribution visualizations characterize data spread. Histograms show frequency distribution—for instance, distribution of session durations reveals whether most sessions brief atau extended, dan whether distribution normal atau skewed. Box plots display quartiles dan outliers, useful untuk comparing distributions across groups (response time distributions by server) dan identifying variability. Violin plots combine box plot information dengan density curve, showing shape of distribution.

Comparative visualizations enable benchmarking dan segmentation analysis. Bar charts compare values across categories—error counts by module, usage by department, conversion rates by user segment. Grouped or stacked bars show multi-dimensional comparisons. Bullet charts compare actual values against targets atau benchmarks—for instance, actual response time versus SLA threshold. Small multiples (grid of similar charts for different subsets) enable pattern comparison across many dimensions simultaneously.

Relational visualizations map connections dan flows. Sankey diagrams show flows through process stages—how users move through application flows, where they drop off, volume at each stage. Network graphs display relationships—service dependencies dalam microservices architecture, user collaboration networks based on shared resource access, atau error propagation across components. Chord diagrams show inter-relationships between entities dalam circular layout.

Geospatial visualizations map data to geographic locations. Choropleth maps color regions by metric values—user density by province, transaction volumes by city, system access by country. Point maps plot individual events atau locations. Heat maps show concentration—clustering of users atau activities dalam geographic areas. Particularly relevant untuk systems dengan distributed user bases atau geographic service variations.

Real-time dashboards aggregate multiple visualizations untuk continuous monitoring. Dashboard design principles: prioritize most critical metrics (top-left placement), use consistent color coding (red for errors/alerts, green for healthy), provide drill-down capabilities (clicking chart shows detail), dan enable time range selection. Dashboards may combine: summary metrics (KPIs as big numbers), trend lines (showing trajectory), alerts (highlighting thresholds exceeded), dan detailed breakdowns (tables or charts).

Interactive visualizations enable exploration. Filtering controls allow users to focus on specific time ranges, user segments, atau system components. Zoom dan pan capabilities enable navigating large datasets—zooming into specific time window dalam time-series, panning across different date ranges. Tooltips provide details on demand—hovering over data points reveals exact values, context, atau drill-down options. Linked visualizations update together—selecting segment dalam one chart filters others.

Tools for log visualization range from business intelligence platforms (Tableau, Power BI, Qlik) yang offer drag-and-drop interface dan extensive chart libraries; specialized log analytics platforms (Kibana part of ELK stack, Splunk, Grafana) optimized untuk log data dengan pre-built dashboards; programming libraries (Python's Matplotlib, Seaborn, Plotly; R's ggplot2; JavaScript's D3.js) offering maximum customization; to cloud-native tools (AWS CloudWatch Dashboards, Google Cloud Console, Azure Monitor) integrated dengan cloud services.

Visualization best practices include: choosing chart types appropriate untuk data (avoid pie charts untuk many categories; use scatter plots untuk correlation; use heatmaps untuk large matrices); maintaining data-ink ratio (minimize decorative elements, maximize information); using color purposefully (sequential palettes untuk ordered data, diverging palettes untuk data with meaningful midpoint, categorical palettes untuk distinct categories); ensuring accessibility (colorblind-friendly palettes, sufficient contrast, text alternatives); dan providing context (clear titles, axis labels, legends, annotations explaining significant features).

Dalam evaluasi sistem supply chain management, visualization dashboard might include: line chart showing daily order volumes over past quarter dengan forecast trend; stacked area chart decomposing orders by product category to show composition changes; heatmap calendar showing which days had highest system load; box plots comparing order processing times across warehouses to identify performance outliers; Sankey diagram illustrating order flow from placement through fulfillment stages dengan drop-off quantification; geographic map color-coded by regional delivery performance against SLAs; dan alert panel highlighting real-time anomalies (unusual spike dalam order cancellations, performance degradation at specific warehouse). Together, these visualizations provide comprehensive, actionable view of system performance dan usage patterns, enabling data-driven optimization decisions.

## 3.5 Teknik Pengumpulan Data Lainnya

Selain survei, wawancara, observasi, dan analisis log yang telah dibahas secara mendalam, evaluasi sistem informasi dapat memanfaatkan berbagai teknik pengumpulan data tambahan yang masing-masing memiliki kekuatan unik untuk menjawab pertanyaan evaluasi spesifik. Teknik-teknik ini—Focus Group Discussion (FGD), Delphi Method, benchmarking, dan analisis dokumen—sering digunakan sebagai complement terhadap metode primer, memperkaya understanding dengan perspektif yang berbeda, atau addressing gaps yang tidak tercakup oleh metode lain. Pemilihan dan kombinasi teknik harus guided oleh evaluation questions, available resources, dan nature of sistem yang dievaluasi.

### 3.5.1 Focus Group Discussion (FGD)

Focus Group Discussion adalah metode pengumpulan data kualitatif yang melibatkan diskusi terstruktur antara small group of participants (typically 6-12 orang) yang dipandu oleh skilled moderator. Dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, FGD particularly valuable untuk exploring shared perceptions, generating diverse ideas tentang system improvements, understanding group dynamics dalam system adoption, atau building consensus tentang priorities. Interaksi antar participants—agreement, disagreement, building on each other's ideas—generates richer data daripada individual interviews, revealing social dimensions of system experience.

Perencanaan FGD dimulai dengan defining clear objectives—what specific aspects of sistem to be explored, what questions to be answered. Participant selection menggunakan purposive sampling untuk ensure relevant dan diverse perspectives. Homogeneous groups (similar roles, experience levels) may feel more comfortable sharing, facilitating open discussion; heterogeneous groups (mixed roles, perspectives) generate more diverse viewpoints tetapi may experience power dynamics yang inhibit some voices. Number of groups depends on diversity required—typically 3-5 groups untuk capture range of perspectives dan achieve saturation.

Moderator's role critical untuk FGD success. Skilled moderator: introduces discussion objectives dan ground rules (respectful listening, encouraging all voices, maintaining confidentiality); guides conversation through discussion topics using semi-structured protocol; encourages participation dari quieter members; manages dominant participants without shutting them down; probes for deeper exploration or examples; keeps discussion focused dan on-time; dan creates safe environment untuk candid expression. Co-moderator atau observer can assist dengan note-taking, managing recording equipment, atau tracking non-verbal dynamics.

Discussion guide structures conversation typically dalam funnel format: opening questions build rapport dan engagement (easy, general questions like "How long have you been using system?"); introductory questions introduce topic (broader questions like "What has your overall experience been?"); transition questions move toward key issues (more focused like "Tell me about challenges you've encountered"); key questions address core evaluation topics (specific, detailed questions like "How has system changed your workflow?"); ending questions provide closure (summary questions like "What are most important changes needed?" atau "Anything we haven't discussed?").

Recording dan documentation essential. Video recording captures verbal content dan important nonverbal cues (who speaks, body language, group interactions) tetapi may inhibit some participants. Audio recording less intrusive, captures verbal content untuk transcription. Detailed notes by observer atau moderator supplement recordings, capturing dynamics, seating arrangements, atau contextual observations. Transcription approaches sama dengan interviews—verbatim atau intelligent verbatim, dengan speaker identification dan timestamps.

Analysis of FGD data considers both content (what said) dan interaction (how discussion unfolded). Thematic analysis identifies recurring topics, shared perceptions, dan points of consensus atau contention. Attention to group dynamics reveals whether certain views dominant due to genuine consensus atau due to social pressure, whether conflicting perspectives represent distinct user segments, atau whether ideas build progressively through interaction. Comparing across multiple FGD groups identifies patterns that hold broadly versus group-specific views.

FGD advantages dalam evaluasi sistem informasi include: efficiency (gathering multiple perspectives simultaneously), synergy (participants stimulate each other's thinking, generating ideas that might not emerge individually), observation of social processes (how users collectively make sense of systems, negotiate meanings, atau develop shared workarounds), dan member checking (participants can immediately validate atau challenge each other's interpretations). Limitations include: dominant voices may overshadow others, social desirability bias may suppress negative opinions (especially dalam hierarchical organizational cultures), confidentiality harder to ensure (participants know each other's views), logistical complexity of scheduling groups, dan analysis complexity of group interactions.

Dalam evaluasi sistem hospital information system, FGD might be conducted with separate groups: nurses dari different wards, physicians by specialty, administrative staff, dan pharmacy team. Discussion might explore: how sistem integrates into clinical workflows, communication challenges between departments using different modules, ideas untuk mobile functionality supporting bedside care, training needs for newly hired staff, dan priorities untuk next system upgrade. Physician FGD might reveal frustration dengan e-prescribing workflow, while pharmacy FGD provides complementary perspective on challenges receiving unclear digital orders, leading to design improvements addressing both sides of interface.

### 3.5.2 Delphi Method

Delphi Method adalah structured communication technique designed untuk achieve expert consensus on complex issues through iterative rounds of questionnaires, interspersed dengan controlled feedback. Dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, Delphi particularly useful untuk: establishing evaluation criteria ketika no established standards exist, forecasting future system requirements atau technology trends, prioritizing system features atau improvements, atau assessing risks dan opportunities dalam system strategy. Method leverages collective wisdom of experts sementara mitigating biases dari face-to-face group interactions (like groupthink atau dominance by vocal individuals).

Delphi process typically involves: (1) Panel selection—identifying dan recruiting experts dengan relevant knowledge tentang system domain, technology, atau organizational context; panels typically 10-30 experts untuk balance diversity dengan manageability. (2) Round 1 questionnaire—open-ended questions inviting experts to identify issues, criteria, atau forecasts; responses analyzed untuk extract key themes. (3) Round 2 questionnaire—structured items based on Round 1 responses, asking experts to rate importance, likelihood, atau agreement on each item; summary statistics (median, interquartile range) calculated untuk group responses. (4) Controlled feedback—participants receive anonymized summary of group responses dan their own previous responses, showing areas of consensus dan divergence. (5) Round 3 (dan potentially subsequent rounds)—experts reconsider ratings dalam light of group feedback, either confirming their views atau revising toward consensus; process continues until acceptable level of consensus reached atau stability achieved (responses no longer changing significantly).

Questionnaire design crucial untuk effective Delphi. Round 1 uses open-ended questions enabling exploration: "What criteria should be used untuk evaluating success of our ERP implementation?" or "What are key risks dalam migrating to cloud-based system?" Analysis of Round 1 requires qualitative coding untuk identify distinct items. Round 2 converts items to rating scales: "Rate importance of each criterion from 1 (not important) to 5 (critically important)." Clear definitions dan examples provided untuk ensure shared understanding. Round 3 presents summary statistics dan asks for reconsideration: "Group median rating was 4, your previous rating was 2—do you wish to revise atau maintain your rating?"

Consensus metrics guide termination decisions. Statistical consensus might be defined as: interquartile range ≤ 1 on 5-point scale (indicating tight agreement), percentage of responses within one point of median ≥ 75% (indicating convergence), atau coefficient of variation below threshold (indicating low relative dispersion). Stability measured by comparing consecutive rounds—if individual responses change by less than predetermined amount (e.g., mean shift < 0.5), process may terminate even without perfect consensus.

Delphi advantages include: anonymity reduces social pressure dan power dynamics; iteration allows experts to refine views dengan consideration of others' perspectives; geographic distribution feasible (no need untuk physical meetings); wide range of expertise can be included; dan resulting consensus has legitimacy dari collective expert judgment. Disadvantages: time-consuming (multiple rounds over weeks atau months); attrition (experts dropping out dalam later rounds); difficulty defining "expert" dan ensuring panel representativeness; potential untuk researcher bias dalam summarizing responses atau defining items; dan consensus may converge on "safe" middle ground rather than innovative ideas.

Modifications of classical Delphi adapt method untuk different contexts. Policy Delphi prioritizes exploring diverse perspectives rather than forcing consensus, useful ketika evaluating contested system decisions. Real-Time Delphi uses online platforms untuk continuous input rather than discrete rounds, enabling faster iteration. Delphi with Face-to-Face Meeting combines anonymous rounds dengan final in-person meeting untuk discuss remaining divergences dan finalize consensus.

Dalam evaluasi sistem e-government, Delphi might be employed untuk establishing comprehensive evaluation framework. Panel of 20 experts—including government officials, IT professionals, public administration researchers, dan citizen representatives—participate. Round 1 asks: "What dimensions of e-government system performance should be evaluated?" Experts identify 45 distinct criteria ranging dari technical (security, uptime) to organizational (staff competency, process integration) to citizen-centric (accessibility, transparency). Round 2 asks experts to rate importance of each criterion; analysis shows consensus on top-tier criteria (security, accessibility, ease of use) but divergence pada others. Round 3 presents feedback; some experts revise ratings after seeing group perspective, resulting dalam refined set of 25 priority criteria dengan strong consensus, providing validated framework untuk subsequent evaluation activities.

### 3.5.3 Benchmarking

Benchmarking adalah systematic process of comparing organization's system performance, processes, atau practices against those of other organizations atau established standards, untuk identify gaps, best practices, dan improvement opportunities. Dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, benchmarking provides external reference points—answering "how good is our system?" requires knowing "compared to what?" Benchmarks can be industry standards, competitors' performance, best-in-class organizations, atau historical performance trends.

Types of benchmarking relevant untuk system evaluation include: Internal benchmarking compares performance across different units within same organization—for instance, comparing system usage rates across regional branches, atau service desk response times across departments; useful untuk identifying internal best practices yang can be transferred. Competitive benchmarking compares against direct competitors—for instance, comparing e-commerce platform features atau transaction processing speeds; provides market positioning insight tetapi competitor data often hard to obtain. Functional benchmarking compares against organizations dalam different industries tetapi dengan similar processes—for instance, comparing online customer service systems across retail, banking, dan healthcare untuk identify universal best practices. Generic benchmarking focuses on fundamental processes (like procurement atau reporting) across any industry.

Benchmarking process typically follows stages: (1) Planning—define what to benchmark (specific systems, processes, metrics), identify benchmark partners atau sources, establish data collection methods. (2) Data collection—gather quantitative metrics (performance indicators, costs, usage statistics) dan qualitative information (process descriptions, technology configurations, organizational practices) dari both own organization dan benchmark sources. (3) Analysis—compare performance levels, identify gaps (positive or negative), investigate root causes of differences. (4) Integration—communicate findings to stakeholders, set performance targets based on benchmarks, develop action plans untuk closing gaps. (5) Action—implement improvements, monitor progress. (6) Maturity—benchmarking becomes ongoing process, dengan periodic updates dan continuous improvement culture.

Metrics untuk benchmarking systems vary by domain. Technical performance metrics: system availability (uptime percentage), response time (transaction atau page load speed), throughput (transactions per second), error rates, recovery time after failures. Operational metrics: cost per transaction, cost per user, IT spend as percentage of revenue, helpdesk tickets per user, resolution time. Usage metrics: adoption rate (percentage of intended users actively using system), feature utilization rate, session frequency dan duration, task completion rate. Outcome metrics: user satisfaction scores, business value delivered (ROI, productivity gains), compliance rates, process cycle time reductions.

Data sources untuk benchmarking include: Industry reports dan studies (from analyst firms like Gartner, Forrester, IDC) providing aggregate statistics dari peer organizations; benchmarking consortia atau associations where organizations voluntarily share metrics; published case studies atau vendor-provided benchmarks (treated cautiously due to potential bias); academic research pada system performance dalam various sectors; atau direct benchmarking partnerships where organizations agree to mutually share data.

Challenges dalam system benchmarking include: comparability issues (differences dalam organization size, industry, technology maturity make direct comparison difficult; normalization techniques necessary); data availability dan reliability (benchmark data may be limited, outdated, atau based on self-reporting with unknown accuracy); contextual differences (what works dalam one organizational culture atau regulatory environment may not transfer); dan focusing on easily measured metrics while neglecting important but harder-to-quantify factors (like system flexibility atau innovation enablement).

Best practices untuk effective benchmarking: focus on processes dan practices, not just metrics (understanding "how" high performers achieve results, not just "what" results they achieve); benchmark against stretch targets (best-in-class) untuk drive ambition, alongside realistic peers untuk practical insights; combine quantitative metrics dengan qualitative understanding of context; ensure internal readiness untuk change (benchmarking useless if organization unwilling to adapt); dan maintain ethical conduct (respecting confidentiality of benchmark partners, properly attributing sources, avoiding misrepresentation).

Dalam evaluasi sistem enterprise resource planning (ERP) di perusahaan manufaktur, benchmarking study might compare against: industry peers (other manufacturing companies of similar size) untuk metrics like inventory turnover improvement post-ERP, order-to-cash cycle time, procurement cost savings; best-in-class organizations (regardless of industry) untuk technical metrics like system uptime (targeting 99.9%), atau user adoption rates (targeting 95%+ active usage); vendor-provided benchmarks untuk understanding typical ROI timelines (commonly 3-5 years untuk full ERP implementations). Analysis reveals organization's system achieves strong technical performance (uptime exceeding peers) tetapi lags dalam adoption (only 70% actively using advanced features) dan business value realization (limited process improvement gains). Investigation uncovers inadequate training dan change management as root causes, leading to targeted intervention programs informed by best practices dari benchmark leaders yang achieved higher adoption.

### 3.5.4 Analisis Dokumen dan Arsip

Analisis dokumen dan arsip adalah examination of existing written atau recorded materials yang relevant untuk system evaluation. Documents provide rich contextual information, historical perspective, dan official records of decisions, policies, atau performance yang complement primary data collection. Dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, documentary sources include: strategic plans dan business cases yang articulated original system justifications; requirements specifications dan design documents describing intended functionality; project plans, meeting minutes, dan status reports chronicling implementation; training materials dan user manuals explaining system features; policies, procedures, dan governance documents guiding system use; audit reports, compliance assessments, dan security reviews; dan performance reports, dashboards, atau analytics summarizing operational metrics.

Document selection requires purposive sampling—identifying documents that most relevant untuk evaluation questions. Sampling strategies include: exhaustive sampling (examining all documents dalam defined category, feasible untuk limited sets); typical case sampling (documents representing normal situations); critical case sampling (documents related to key decisions atau turning points); atau maximum variation sampling (diverse document types untuk comprehensive view). Access negotiations may be necessary—some documents confidential atau require permissions; sensitivity to proprietary atau personal information essential.

Document characteristics influence their utility dan interpretation. Authenticity—is document genuine dan from claimed source? Credibility—is author trustworthy dan information accurate? Representativeness—is document typical atau exceptional? Meaning—what was intended message, dan what can be inferred? Context matters enormously: organizational documents serve specific purposes (justification, accountability, persuasion) yang shape their content; reading documents requires understanding their function within organizational culture dan politics.

Analytical approaches untuk documents include: Content analysis—systematic coding of document content untuk identify themes, count frequencies, atau test hypotheses; can be quantitative (counting mentions of topics) atau qualitative (interpreting meanings). Thematic analysis—identifying patterns dan themes across documents, similar to interview analysis. Critical discourse analysis—examining how language used dalam documents reflects dan shapes power relations, ideologies, atau constructions of reality; particularly relevant untuk understanding how systems framed atau legitimated. Historical analysis—tracing evolution of ideas, decisions, atau policies through chronological sequence of documents.

Document evidence triangulated dengan other data sources strengthens evaluation. For instance, system requirements document states intended features; user interviews reveal which features actually used; system logs provide objective usage data. Convergence across sources increases confidence; divergence signals important gaps—between intent dan reality, espoused dan actual practices, official policies dan informal behaviors. Such gaps are often evaluation findings themselves, revealing implementation challenges atau emerging needs.

Practical considerations untuk document analysis: organizing large document sets requires systematic filing dan indexing; digital documents may exist dalam multiple versions—version control important; OCR (Optical Character Recognition) may be necessary untuk converting scanned documents to searchable text; redaction atau anonymization may be required untuk protecting sensitive information dalam reporting; dan citation conventions should properly attribute document sources while respecting confidentiality agreements.

Limitations of documentary evidence: documents reflect perspectives of authors (usually organizational leadership atau technical experts), potentially missing frontline user views; documents may be incomplete, outdated, atau inaccurate; they represent formal face of organization, omitting informal practices; dan they are retrospective, may not capture current state if documentation lags reality.

Dalam evaluasi sistem customer relationship management (CRM), document analysis might examine: original business case projecting 30% increase dalam sales productivity, 20% customer retention improvement, dan 18-month ROI; vendor proposals comparing features of three finalist systems; selection committee minutes revealing key decision criteria (integration dengan existing systems weighted heavily); implementation project plans showing phased rollout schedule; training curriculum showing 8-hour program for sales staff; data governance policy specifying customer data access controls; monthly performance dashboards tracking sales metrics; dan IT audit report identifying security vulnerabilities. Analysis reveals gap between ambitious business case projections (not realized according to performance data) dan modest training investment (potentially contributing to underutilization), corroborated by interview findings of inadequate user competence. Historical tracing shows initial enthusiasm (early documents emphasize transformation potential) giving way to implementation challenges (later documents focus on technical issues, training mentioned minimally), explaining disappointing outcomes. This documentary evidence provides essential context untuk interpreting primary data dan formulating improvement recommendations.

## 3.6 Triangulasi Data

Triangulasi adalah strategi menggunakan multiple data sources, methods, investigators, atau theories untuk develop comprehensive understanding of phenomena yang dievaluasi. Istilah borrowed dari navigation dan surveying, di mana multiple reference points used untuk determine precise position. Dalam evaluasi sistem informasi, triangulasi enhances credibility, validity, dan comprehensiveness of findings dengan converging evidence dari different perspectives. Single-method studies inherently limited—each method has blind spots, biases, atau constraints yang may distort understanding. Triangulasi systematically addresses these limitations melalui methodological diversity.

### 3.6.1 Konsep Triangulasi dalam Evaluasi

Philosophical foundations of triangulasi rest on assumption bahwa reality multifaceted dan no single method dapat fully capture complexity. Each method illuminates certain aspects while obscuring others. Surveys efficiently capture breadth of opinions tetapi miss depth dan context; interviews provide rich narratives tetapi from limited participants; observations reveal actual behaviors tetapi are resource-intensive; logs provide objective traces tetapi lack user intentions. Triangulasi deliberately combines methods untuk offset their respective weaknesses dan build more complete picture.

Purposes of triangulasi include: Convergence atau corroboration—when different methods produce similar findings, confidence dalam conclusions increases; for instance, if surveys show low satisfaction dengan system usability, interviews reveal specific frustration points, dan observational data shows workarounds, convergent evidence strongly supports usability problems requiring attention. Complementarity—different methods address different aspects, together providing fuller understanding; for instance, log data shows what users do, while interviews explain why. Contradiction atau divergence—when methods produce conflicting findings, this signals need untuk deeper investigation of conditions, contexts, atau interpretations that explain differences; contradictions themselves become findings requiring explanation.

Triangulation can occur at different stages of evaluation: Design triangulation—using multiple methods in evaluation design from outset, with planned integration; this approach ensures systematic collection of diverse data. Emergent triangulation—starting dengan one method, then adding others based on initial findings to explore emerging questions or validate unexpected results; this flexible approach responds to evaluation evolution. Meta-triangulation—synthesizing findings across separate studies atau evaluation efforts untuk build cumulative knowledge.

Triangulation doesn't assume single "truth" exists waiting to be discovered. In constructivist perspectives, multiple valid interpretations possible; triangulation explores diversity of meanings rather than seeking singular reality. In pragmatic perspectives (common dalam applied evaluation), triangulation balances pursuit of objectivity dengan recognition of multiple stakeholder perspectives, aiming for actionable insights that account untuk complexity.

### 3.6.2 Jenis-jenis Triangulasi

Data triangulation uses multiple data sources untuk study same phenomenon. Sources can vary by: time (collecting data at different time points untuk assess stability atau change—baseline, mid-implementation, post-implementation evaluations), space (data dari different locations—multiple branch offices, different departments), atau person (data dari different stakeholders—end users, administrators, managers, customers). For instance, evaluating email system might collect data dari: frontline employees (ease of use, reliability), IT helpdesk (support tickets, common issues), managers (communication effectiveness, collaboration impacts), dan external partners (integration experiences). Convergence across sources strengthens findings; divergence reveals different realities atau priorities worth investigating.

Methodological triangulation combines different research methods. Within-method triangulation uses variations within same approach—for instance, using multiple survey instruments atau conducting different types of interviews (structured, semi-structured, life history). Between-method triangulation combines fundamentally different methods—typically mixing qualitative dan quantitative approaches. Classic combination dalam system evaluation: quantitative surveys measuring satisfaction broadly, qualitative interviews exploring reasons behind satisfaction ratings, observational data capturing actual usage behaviors, dan system logs providing objective performance metrics. Each method's strengths compensate for others' limitations.

Investigator triangulation involves multiple researchers atau evaluators collecting dan analyzing data. Different observers bring different perspectives, backgrounds, dan biases; comparison of their independent findings reveals areas of consensus dan interpretive differences. Particularly valuable dalam qualitative analysis where interpretation plays significant role. For instance, independent coding of interview transcripts by two researchers, followed by discussion to resolve discrepancies, enhances reliability. Team-based evaluation dengan diverse expertise (technical specialists, social scientists, domain experts) enables multidisciplinary perspectives.

Theory triangulation applies multiple theoretical frameworks atau perspectives untuk interpreting data. Different theories illuminate different aspects—for instance, technology acceptance model (TAM) might explain adoption based on perceived usefulness dan ease of use, while structuration theory might reveal how system shapes dan is shaped by organizational practices, dan actor-network theory might trace how human dan non-human actors (users, technologies, policies) form networks of relations. Multiple lenses avoid theoretical bias dari single perspective, revealing richer, more nuanced understanding.

Environmental triangulation varies situational factors—examining phenomena under different conditions atau settings. For instance, evaluating mobile system might observe usage dalam office environments dan field conditions, during normal operations dan crisis situations, atau with novice versus experienced users. Variations reveal how context shapes experiences, identifying boundary conditions atau moderating factors affecting system effectiveness.

### 3.6.3 Implementasi Triangulasi Data

Implementing triangulation requires thoughtful planning and systematic execution. Planning phase defines: evaluation questions that will be addressed (clear questions guide method selection); methods to be used dan rationale for each (why this combination addresses questions effectively); sequence dan timing (concurrent data collection allows comparison; sequential allows each method to inform next); integration points (where dan how findings will be combined); dan resource allocation (time, budget, personnel for each method).

Data collection phase executes multiple methods, maintaining quality standards for each. Important considerations: If methods concurrent, ensure consistency dalam timeframes so phenomena observed at similar points. If sequential, plan how initial findings inform subsequent data collection—for instance, survey results identifying problematic modules can guide targeted interview sampling. Maintain separate documentation for each method—clear data trails essential for later integration. Ensure ethical protocols consistently applied—informed consent, confidentiality protections—across all methods.

Analysis phase can proceed sequentially (completing analysis of each method before integration) atau concurrently (iterative analysis informing ongoing collection). Triangulation protocols guide systematic comparison: Create comparison matrices—rows representing themes atau findings, columns representing methods, cells containing relevant evidence from each method. Code convergence dan divergence—mark findings that appear across methods (convergent), appear dalam only one method (unique), atau contradict across methods (divergent). Seek explanations—when findings converge, this supports robustness; when divergent, investigate whether this reflects different aspects of reality, methodological artifacts, atau genuinely contradictory evidence requiring reconciliation.

Integration of findings synthesizes insights across methods. Approaches include: Data transformation—converting qualitative findings to quantitative (counting themes) atau quantitative to qualitative (creating narratives from patterns) untuk enable direct comparison. Narrative weaving—constructing integrated story that draws upon different data sources, showing how they complement each other. Joint display—visual representations (tables, diagrams) showing how different datasets address evaluation questions dan relate to each other. Meta-matrix—comprehensive display organizing all findings by themes, data sources, dan strength of evidence.

Reconciling contradictions when methods yield inconsistent findings requires investigation rather than dismissal. Possible explanations: Methodological artifacts—limitations of methods produce apparent contradictions (e.g., social desirability bias dalam surveys yields more positive responses than candid interviews). Temporal differences—if data collected at different times, changes may explain differences. Sampling differences—different methods may access different subpopulations dengan legitimately different experiences. Contextual factors—methods capture phenomena dalam different contexts. Genuine complexity—phenomenon truly contradictory, reflecting paradoxes atau tensions within system reality. Careful examination determines which explanation applies, often yielding important insights.

Quality criteria for triangulation: Coherence—do integrated findings form logical, consistent interpretation? Comprehensiveness—does synthesis address full range of evaluation questions? Transparency—are data sources, methods, dan integration processes clearly documented? Reflexivity—have evaluators reflected on how their decisions shaped integration? Utility—does triangulation yield actionable insights beyond what single method would provide?

### 3.6.4 Manfaat dan Tantangan Triangulasi

Manfaat triangulasi dalam evaluasi sistem informasi substantial. Enhanced credibility—findings supported by multiple methods command greater confidence from stakeholders; particularly important untuk high-stakes decisions about significant system investments atau changes. Increased validity—convergent evidence from diverse sources strengthens conclusions; divergent evidence prompts deeper investigation, either revealing important nuances atau identifying spurious findings. Comprehensive understanding—different methods access different aspects of system reality (objective behaviors, subjective experiences, social dynamics, technical performance), yielding holistic picture that informs better decisions.

Triangulasi reveals unexpected insights that single methods miss. Contradictions between methods often most illuminating—for instance, surveys showing high satisfaction but logs showing low usage suggests response bias atau misunderstanding of questions; interviews praising system but observations revealing extensive workarounds indicates gap between espoused dan actual practice. Such findings drive productive inquiry into underlying causes.

Methodological learning occurs—experience dengan multiple approaches builds evaluator capabilities. Stakeholder engagement often enhanced because diverse methods access different stakeholder groups, ensuring broader participation. Policy dan practical implications strengthened—recommendations based on triangulated evidence more robust, addressing multiple dimensions of problems identified through various methods.

Tantangan implementing triangulasi include: Resource intensiveness—multiple methods require more time, money, dan expertise than single method; trade-offs dengan comprehensiveness must be navigated. Complexity dalam design dan execution—coordinating multiple methods, ensuring quality for each, managing data dari diverse sources demands sophisticated project management. Skills requirements—evaluators need competence across methods or access to multidisciplinary teams; not all evaluators equally skilled dalam qualitative dan quantitative approaches.

Integration challenges arise—combining fundamentally different data types (numbers, texts, images, behaviors) requires conceptual dan technical sophistication. Epistemological tensions may emerge—team members atau stakeholders dengan commitments to particular paradigms (positivist, interpretivist) may resist integration. Potential untuk confirmation bias—evaluators may emphasize convergent evidence supporting preconceptions while downplaying divergent findings.

Timing challenges complicate sequential designs—delays dalam initial method affect downstream schedule; emergent sampling (using initial findings to guide subsequent methods) introduces uncertainties. Quality trade-offs may occur—pressure to cover multiple methods may compromise depth or rigor of each; better to do fewer methods well than many poorly.

Mitigation strategies include: realistic scoping—match triangulation ambition to available resources, prioritizing most critical evaluation questions; phased approaches—if full triangulation infeasible initially, start dengan core methods dan add others as resources allow; collaborative partnerships—leverage external expertise atau partners with complementary capabilities; methodological training—build team capacity through training in diverse methods; explicit protocols—develop clear procedures for data integration, conflict resolution, dan quality assurance; dan transparent reporting—clearly document what methods used, how integrated, what convergences atau divergences found, dan how these interpreted.

Dalam evaluasi komprehensif sistem electronic medical records (EMR) di rumah sakit besar, triangulasi might involve: (1) Survey of 500 healthcare providers measuring satisfaction, perceived usability, dan impact on clinical efficiency—provides breadth of quantitative evidence across user base. (2) Semi-structured interviews dengan 30 purposively sampled physicians, nurses, dan administrative staff exploring workflow integration, training adequacy, dan specific challenges—provides depth dan contextual understanding. (3) Observational study dalam 5 clinical units documenting actual EMR usage during patient care, workarounds developed, dan communication patterns—reveals practices that may differ dari self-reports. (4) Analysis of system logs tracking login frequencies, feature utilization, documentation times, dan error rates—provides objective behavioral data. (5) Review of clinical quality metrics (medication error rates, documentation completeness, turnaround times) pre- dan post-EMR implementation—assesses outcome impacts.

Integration reveals: Convergence—all methods indicate steep learning curve dan initial productivity dip, dengan recovery period of 4-6 months; strong evidence supports need untuk extended training dan go-live support. Complementarity—surveys show overall satisfaction dengan alerts dan decision support (quantitative breadth); interviews explain how alerts improve safety but create alert fatigue (qualitative depth); observations reveal providers developing routines untuk managing alerts efficiently (behavioral practices); logs quantify alert override rates shedding light on fatigue extent (objective metrics). Contradiction—surveys show high satisfaction dengan mobile access (mean 4.2/5), but logs show very low mobile app usage (<10% of users); investigation through follow-up interviews reveals users satisfied dengan idea of mobile access (aspirational response) but face practical barriers (device policies, connectivity); resolves apparent contradiction dan identifies implementation gap.

Final integrated findings, supported by triangulated evidence, inform recommendations: enhance training program (supported by all methods showing learning challenges), optimize alert system (combining satisfaction data, fatigue concerns, override patterns), dan address mobile implementation barriers (explaining satisfaction-usage gap). Stakeholders—hospital leadership, clinical staff, IT department—find recommendations credible because grounded dalam diverse evidence types, each compelling to different audiences (administrators value outcome metrics, clinicians value observational realism, IT values log data objectivity). Triangulasi thus enhances both validity of findings dan effectiveness of evaluation dalam driving meaningful system improvements.

## Ringkasan Bab

Bab ini telah menguraikan teknik-teknik pengumpulan data fundamental yang membentuk toolbox evaluator sistem informasi. Instrumen survei memberikan kemampuan untuk mengumpulkan data terstruktur dari sampel besar secara efisien, mengukur kepuasan, usability, dan perceived quality dengan rigor statistik. Keefektifan survei bergantung pada desain yang cermat—operasionalisasi konstruk yang valid, pertanyaan yang well-crafted, sampling yang representatif, dan analisis yang appropriate—serta awareness terhadap keterbatasan inheren seperti response bias dan surface-level understanding.

Wawancara terstruktur melengkapi survei dengan kemampuan untuk menggali depth, context, dan nuance yang tidak dapat ditangkap oleh closed-ended questions. Dari perencanaan purposive sampling hingga skilled moderation yang mengelola dynamics, dari systematic transcription hingga rigorous thematic analysis, wawancara menghasilkan rich narratives tentang user experiences, organizational realities, dan system impacts. Semi-structured approaches menyeimbangkan consistency dengan flexibility, memungkinkan both systematic coverage dan emergent discovery.

Observasi partisipatif memberikan akses langsung kepada actual practices, contextual factors, dan tacit knowledge yang users mungkin tidak dapat artikulasikan. Dengan varying levels of participation, observers dapat witness bagaimana systems actually used dalam natural settings, mengidentifikasi gaps antara intended dan actual use, dan memahami socio-technical dynamics. Observasi demands careful negotiation of roles, systematic field note practices, dan reflexive awareness tentang observer impacts, sementara offering unparalleled insights into work realities.

Analisis log sistem memanfaatkan digital traces untuk objective, comprehensive, dan fine-grained understanding of system behaviors dan user interactions. Dari access logs hingga error logs, dari transaction patterns hingga performance metrics, log analysis reveals usage patterns, identifies anomalies, dan quantifies performance characteristics that complement perceptual data. Namun, transforming raw logs into insights requires technical skills dalam extraction, cleaning, pattern analysis, dan visualization, serta contextual understanding untuk meaningful interpretation.

Teknik tambahan—FGD untuk exploring shared perceptions dan group dynamics, Delphi untuk achieving expert consensus, benchmarking untuk external comparison dan best practice identification, dan document analysis untuk historical context dan official records—expand evaluator's repertoire. Setiap teknik addresses specific evaluation needs dan information gaps.

Triangulasi mengintegrasikan multiple methods, sources, investigators, atau theories untuk enhanced credibility, comprehensive understanding, dan discovery of unexpected insights through convergence, complementarity, atau productive contradictions. Sementara resource-intensive dan complex, triangulasi delivers robust findings yang support confident decision-making. Effective implementation requires thoughtful planning, systematic execution, careful integration, dan transparent reporting.

Pemilihan teknik pengumpulan data harus guided oleh evaluation questions, resource constraints, stakeholder needs, dan characteristics of sistem yang dievaluasi. No single technique sufficient untuk comprehensive evaluation; strategic combination, carefully executed dan integrated, yields valid, credible, dan useful insights yang inform system improvements dan organizational decisions.

## Studi Kasus Diskusi Kelas: Evaluasi Sistem Telemedicine Nasional

### Konteks

Kementerian Kesehatan meluncurkan sistem telemedicine nasional "SehatDigital" untuk memperluas akses layanan kesehatan ke daerah terpencil. Sistem ini memungkinkan konsultasi video antara pasien di puskesmas dengan dokter spesialis di rumah sakit rujukan, berbagi rekam medis elektronik, dan e-prescription. Setelah 18 bulan implementasi di 500 puskesmas across 20 provinsi, evaluasi komprehensif diperlukan untuk menginformasikan scaling decisions dan improvement priorities.

**Stakeholders dengan Potentially Different Perspectives:**
- **Pasien di daerah terpencil**: concern tentang quality of care via telemedicine versus in-person, ease of access, privacy
- **Tenaga kesehatan puskesmas**: workload impacts, technical challenges, training adequacy, professional autonomy
- **Dokter spesialis**: efficiency of virtual consultations, clinical information adequacy, medico-legal concerns
- **Administrators puskesmas**: operational costs, staff acceptance, integration dengan existing workflows
- **Kementerian Kesehatan**: cost-effectiveness, health outcomes improvement, equity of access, political visibility
- **Provider teknologi**: system performance, user satisfaction, contract renewal prospects

**System Characteristics:**
- Distributed implementation across diverse geographic dan socio-economic contexts (urban vs. remote, Java vs. Eastern Indonesia)
- High variability dalam infrastructure quality (internet connectivity ranges dari fiber to 3G)
- Mixed user technical literacy (ranging dari digital native young nurses to senior doctors unfamiliar dengan telemedicine)
- Complex integration requirements (with existing hospital information systems, national health insurance system, pharmaceutical supply chain)
- Critical implications (healthcare quality, patient safety, health equity)

**Evaluation Constraints:**
- Timeline: 6 months untuk complete evaluation dan deliver recommendations
- Budget: Rp 2 miliar (approximately USD 135,000)
- Team: 5 evaluators dengan mixed expertise (public health, information systems, qualitative research, data analytics)
- Political sensitivity: high-profile government program; findings akan inform major policy decisions dan budget allocations

### Pertanyaan Diskusi

**Kelompok 1 - Data Collection Strategy Design:**

Develop comprehensive data collection strategy untuk this evaluation. Address:
- Which techniques (dari Bab 3) akan you prioritize, dan why? Justify selections based on evaluation questions, stakeholder needs, context characteristics, dan resource constraints.
- How will you ensure representativeness across diverse implementation contexts (geography, infrastructure, user types)? Design sampling strategy.
- How will you handle linguistic diversity (Bahasa Indonesia, regional languages, medical terminology)? Consider implications untuk surveys, interviews, training of data collectors.
- What specific ethical considerations arise dalam healthcare context (patient privacy, clinical data sensitivity, power dynamics between providers)? Propose safeguards.
- Develop timeline showing sequence dan parallelization of data collection activities within 6-month window.

**Kelompok 2 - Triangulasi Implementation:**

Design triangulation approach untuk answering critical evaluation question: "Does SehatDigital improve healthcare access dan quality untuk underserved populations?" Address:
- Identify minimal three different data sources atau methods yang will provide evidence untuk this question. Untuk each, specify: what it will measure/capture, why it's appropriate, what limitations it has.
- Develop integration framework: how will you systematically compare findings across methods? Create sample matrix atau visualization showing how different data types akan be juxtaposed.
- Anticipate potential contradictions: what if patient satisfaction surveys show high ratings, but clinical outcome data shows no improvement? How would you investigate dan interpret such divergence?
- Discuss how triangulation addresses political sensitivity—how does multi-method approach strengthen credibility of findings dalam contested policy environment?

**Kelompok 3 - Log Analysis Strategy:**

SehatDigital system generates extensive logs: session logs (logins, duration, disconnections), clinical transaction logs (consultations conducted, diagnoses recorded, prescriptions issued), technical performance logs (bandwidth, latency, errors), dan audit logs (access to patient records). Design log analysis component addressing:
- Which log types akan you prioritize untuk extraction dan analysis? Justify based on evaluation priorities.
- What specific metrics atau patterns akan you derive dari logs? (e.g., adoption rate, consultation completion rate, technical failure rate, usage patterns by geography/time)
- How will you handle data quality issues (missing logs dari sites dengan poor connectivity, inconsistent data formats across implementations, potential for gaming metrics)?
- What visualizations akan you create untuk communicate log-based findings to non-technical stakeholders (ministry officials, healthcare providers)?
- How will you triangulate log data dengan other sources? (e.g., comparing logged consultation durations dengan provider perceptions of adequacy, atau system uptime logs dengan user-reported reliability)
- Address privacy dan security: patient health information dalam logs requires careful handling; what de-identification atau aggregation strategies necessary?

**Kelompok 4 - Methodological Adaptation untuk Context:**

Indonesia's diversity presents unique challenges. Discuss adaptations untuk data collection techniques:
- **Survey administration**: How deliver surveys to users dalam remote areas dengan limited internet? Paper-based, mobile-based, interviewer-administered? Trade-offs of each approach?
- **Interview logistics**: Conducting interviews across archipelago—mix of in-person (where? how select sites untuk visits?) dan virtual (but how ensure representativeness given connectivity barriers)? How build trust dalam brief encounters dengan diverse cultural groups?
- **Observasi feasibility**: Observing telemedicine consultations raises patient consent issues dan may affect clinical interactions. How minimize intrusiveness while capturing authentic practices? Video recording, note-taking, atau structured observation protocols?
- **FGD cultural adaptation**: Hierarchical culture may inhibit junior staff speaking candidly dalam mixed groups. How constitute groups untuk encourage openness? Separate sessions by role? How facilitate discussions dalam culturally appropriate ways?
- Propose practical solutions untuk these challenges that maintain methodological rigor while respecting contextual realities.

**Plenary Discussion:**

After group presentations, facilitate discussion on:
- How would evaluation approach differ if this were private-sector telehealth startup (rather than government program)? Consider different stakeholders, evaluation questions, data access, success criteria.
- What if timeline compressed to 3 months (ministerial directive untuk rapid assessment)? What trade-offs in comprehensiveness, depth, rigor would be necessary?
- How ensure evaluation findings actually utilized untuk improvement (not just filed)? Discuss stakeholder engagement strategies, communication of findings, actionable recommendations.
- Reflect on evaluator's role dalam politically sensitive context: balancing objectivity dengan awareness of political dynamics, presenting findings that may contradict official narratives, navigating pressures for particular conclusions.
